# Character Skills

## Purpose

Use this summary to choose, record, and improve character skills during character creation and advancement. It covers skill fields, linked attributes, target numbers, complexity ratings, subskills, specialties, tiered skills, and training dependencies.

## When to Use

- A player is selecting skills from Life Modules, skill fields, or points-only purchases.
- The GM needs to review whether a character has the right training for a concept or campaign role.
- A character sheet needs missing subskills, specialties, target data, or training dependencies identified.
- A character wants to learn or improve a skill after play begins.

## Do Not Use For

- Resolving a skill check in play. Use `rules/core/skill-checks.md` after choosing the relevant skill.
- Copying the master skill list, skill field lists, skill descriptions, or exact XP cost table into committed files.
- Replacing specialized combat, equipment, vehicle, medical, tactical, or campaign summaries.
- Deriving Classic BattleTech pilot/gunnery ratings before vehicle and MechWarrior summaries are source-reviewed.

## Basic Procedure

1. Identify whether the skill comes from a Life Module, a Stage 3 skill field, direct points-only purchase, leftover XP, post-creation training, or downtime.
2. Record the exact skill name and any required subskill. A subskill is a separate discipline and should have its own sheet entry.
3. Record any specialty in parentheses only after GM approval. A specialty narrows the skill for a bonus in its focus and a penalty outside that focus.
4. Record linked attributes, target number, and complexity from the private source list.
5. For tiered skills, check whether the current level uses the basic tier or advanced tier. The linked attributes and complexity can change at the threshold.
6. In points-only creation, buy skills and any skill fields from the XP pool while respecting prerequisites, mandatory language/perception purchases, field limits, and field rebates from the source pages.
7. During play, learn or improve skills by spending XP and meeting training or use requirements. Advanced skills need successful training support.
8. Route actual action checks through `rules/core/skill-checks.md` and the relevant subsystem summary.

## Practical GM Guidance

- Let the player's fictional approach choose the skill. A locked door could be Security Systems, Technician, brute force, social engineering, or a forged credential depending on the plan.
- Skill Fields are useful for quickly checking whether a character's role is coherent: soldier, medic, technician, pilot, scout, investigator, officer, scientist, and so on.
- Require subskills where the source does. `Piloting/Mech`, `Gunnery/Mech`, `Navigation/Space`, and `Technician/Myomer` are different practical competencies.
- Keep specialties narrow enough to matter but not so narrow they never appear.
- Do not call for a skill roll when the task is routine, safe, and within the character's basic competence.

## Common Edge Cases

- A character may have XP toward a skill but not enough for the next level. Record the XP without treating the higher level as active.
- Some skills are tiered. A low-level character can use the basic form, but higher levels represent more formal or sophisticated training.
- Language skills often do not need rolls; level mainly indicates comprehension and fluency. Check the Illiterate trait and source pages when reading or writing matters.
- Advanced skills and high-level improvements may require a trainer, time, facilities, and successful Training checks.
- Vehicle, BattleMech, aerospace, battle armor, and tactical skills may need handoff to tactical systems when exact unit combat matters.
