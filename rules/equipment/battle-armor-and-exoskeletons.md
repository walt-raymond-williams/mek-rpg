# Battle Armor And Exoskeletons

## Purpose

Use this summary when a character wears, obtains, dons, exits, fights near, or records battle armor, PA(L), exoskeletons, or related armored suits.

## When to Use

- A PC or NPC has access to battle armor, power armor, exoskeletons, or industrial powered suits.
- The GM needs to decide whether an armored suit is personal gear, tactical equipment, or a campaign asset.
- Donning, emergency egress, suit damage, mounted weapons, manipulators, or battlesuit operation matters.
- Battle armor interacts with ordinary infantry, creatures, vehicles, BattleMechs, or tactical encounter prep.

## Do Not Use For

- Reproducing battle armor tables, BAR conversions, suit stat blocks, movement ratings, mounted weapon values, manipulator tables, or Technical Readout entries.
- Full battle armor tactical combat. Use Total Warfare, Tactical Operations, MegaMek, MekHQ, or `gm/switch-to-classic-battletech.md`.
- Personal armor values already covered by `rules/equipment/armor.md`.
- Treating battle armor as ordinary clothing or a simple armor bonus.

## Basic Procedure

1. Identify the suit category: exoskeleton, PA(L), battle armor, or other powered armor.
2. Determine ownership/access: military issue, faction asset, salvage, black market, employer loan, personal property, or unavailable restricted equipment.
3. Decide scene scale:
   - personal combat when battle armor interacts with ordinary infantry or characters
   - tactical handling when battle armor attacks or is attacked by vehicles, BattleMechs, other battle armor, or tactical units
4. If donning or exiting matters, use the source procedure for assisted/unassisted suit entry and emergency egress.
5. Record required skill and support facts: Piloting/Battlesuit, Gunnery/Battlesuit, Small Arms/Support Weapons for handheld gear, Technician support, power/ammo, repair state, and tactical armor.
6. If exact movement, weapons, armor conversion, manipulator effects, or suit damage matters, use the source or tactical tool.
7. After use, update `assets.md`, `pcs.md`, or `npcs.md` with suit location, condition, owner, authorization, damage, ammunition, power, and unresolved lookups.

## Practical GM Guidance

- Battle armor is a force multiplier and a logistics object. Track who owns it, who can legally wear it, who can maintain it, and what faction notices it.
- The key boundary is target scale. Against infantry, personal combat often applies; against vehicles and armored units, tactical rules usually apply.
- Suit operation is not automatic. Piloting/Battlesuit, Gunnery/Battlesuit, and appropriate personal weapon skills can all matter.
- Donning time matters in ambushes, drops, boarding actions, and emergency scrambles.
- A damaged suit can create rescue, recovery, repair, and ownership consequences even when the tactical fight is over.

## Common Edge Cases

- A suit weapon mounted to the armor may use a different skill and damage procedure than a rifle held in armored gloves.
- Emergency egress depends on the suit still functioning. If the suit is dead or immobilized, rescue may become a scene.
- Exoskeletons may provide strength or mobility support without making the wearer a battlefield unit.
- Battle armor stealth, movement, jump, and melee details are tactical/source lookups.
- If a suit is part of a MekHQ-linked force, MekHQ owns the hard ledger facts; MEK-RPG records RPG overlays and pending intents.
