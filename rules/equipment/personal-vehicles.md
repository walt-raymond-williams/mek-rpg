# Personal Vehicles And Fuel

## Purpose

Use this summary when a character buys, borrows, fuels, records, drives, rides in, repairs, or plans around personal vehicles and common support vehicles.

## When to Use

- A PC wants a car, motorcycle, hovercraft, aircar, truck, bus, snow vehicle, boat, yacht, or similar personal-scale vehicle.
- A mission involves local transport, convoy needs, fuel access, passengers, cargo, route planning, or vehicle availability.
- The campaign needs to record a personal vehicle as an asset with owner, location, condition, fuel, cargo, and crew/passenger notes.
- A vehicle scene might become tactical because armor, speed, motive type, weapons, or damage matters.

## Do Not Use For

- Copying vehicle lists, vehicle stat blocks, fuel tables, costs, cargo values, armor values, or speed/range tables.
- Full tactical combat or vehicle construction. Use Classic BattleTech, Total Warfare, Tactical Operations, MegaMek, MekHQ, or `gm/switch-to-classic-battletech.md`.
- DropShip, large spacecraft, force transport economy, or definitive title/lien law.
- Treating a personal vehicle purchase as a hard ledger fact in MekHQ-linked campaigns unless MekHQ or table canon confirms it.

## Basic Procedure

1. Identify the vehicle role: commuter, luxury, utility, off-road, hover/WiGE, air, water, cargo, passenger, emergency, snow, military, or special purpose.
2. Decide access route: normal purchase, rental, borrow, requisition, salvage, employer-provided, black market, theft, or patron favor.
3. Look up exact table values only when they matter: cost, availability, legality, crew/passengers, cargo, armor, fuel type, range, speed, notes, affiliation, and special equipment.
4. Record the vehicle as a campaign asset if it persists beyond the scene.
5. Track fuel only at the level the campaign needs: ignored for routine local travel, abstract readiness clock for missions, or source/table lookup for exact kg/cost/range.
6. If the vehicle enters combat or tactical movement, decide whether the scene stays as one RPG check or hands off to tactical rules.
7. Update campaign files after use: damage, location, fuel/readiness, impound risk, ownership question, passengers, cargo, and repair needs.

## Practical GM Guidance

- Personal vehicles are more than equipment rows. They create mobility, exposure, legal visibility, maintenance needs, and logistical choices.
- Fuel availability varies by world, region, and engine type. Use scarcity as a mission clock only when it creates meaningful decisions.
- Civilian vehicles are usually not armored tactical assets. Do not let a table entry substitute for a tactical unit record when combat matters.
- A vehicle can be a quiet social signal: luxury, military, industrial, local, offworld, illegal, or conspicuous.
- If the exact vehicle does not matter, choose a class and record a placeholder model with `Needs source lookup` rather than stopping play.

## Common Edge Cases

- Some vehicle table entries are examples of classes, not exhaustive catalog coverage.
- Aircraft may use thrust/range concepts that do not map to road vehicle speed/range; source lookup is required for exact handling.
- Fuel type, battery recharge, and fusion/fission maintenance are different logistics questions.
- A vehicle's armor, motive type, and tactical status make it a vehicular unit in tactical play.
- For MekHQ-linked campaigns, record a pending intent if a vehicle purchase should affect hard funds or force inventory.
