# Personal Gear

## Purpose

Use this summary for non-weapon personal equipment: power packs, chargers, repair and salvage tools, survival gear, mobility gear, security and espionage equipment, and general mission supplies.

## When to Use

- A character needs tools, power, survival supplies, climbing or descent gear, disguise and forgery equipment, security bypass gear, forensic tools, or other mission gear.
- The GM needs to know whether missing tools block a task, impose a modifier, or simply make the task slower.
- A device needs a power source, recharge time, replacement pack, or field maintenance.
- A scene involves locks, evidence, sabotage, field repair, salvage, shelter, navigation, or hostile terrain.

## Do Not Use For

- Exact item values, table bonuses, power capacities, generation rates, costs, masses, ranges, or table notes. Inspect the cited private source pages.
- Medical equipment. Use `rules/equipment/medical-gear.md`.
- Electronics, sensors, optics, and communications. Use `rules/equipment/electronics.md`.
- BattleMech, vehicle, aerospace, or strategic repair systems.

## Basic Procedure

1. Identify the gear function: power, repair, salvage, survival, mobility, security, espionage, forgery, forensics, navigation, shelter, or emergency signaling.
2. If exact equipment stats matter, look up the private table entry for cost, mass, power, capacity, range, bonus, or special notes.
3. Decide whether the task requires tools. If it does, verify whether the character has a basic, deluxe, specialized, improvised, or missing tool set.
4. Apply the relevant core skill procedure: Technician, Security Systems, Survival, Forgery, Disguise, Forensics, Acrobatics, Free-Fall, or another appropriate skill.
5. For powered devices, track compatible power pack size, remaining charge, recharge access, and whether field charging is possible.
6. For mobility and survival gear, state the environmental risk before rolling: falling, exposure, darkness, water, vacuum, weather, lack of food, or failed navigation.
7. For security or espionage equipment, decide whether the gear creates access, information, concealment, authentication, or evidence, then resolve opposition normally.

## Practical GM Guidance

- Tools are best used to open or close options. The right kit can make a task possible, faster, or safer; the wrong kit can force improvisation.
- Keep gear modifiers bounded. If a device seems to solve everything, narrow it to the specific problem it was built for.
- Power is a useful clock. A mission with limited packs, chargers, or grid access has natural pressure without extra bookkeeping.
- Espionage equipment should create scenes, not skip them. A lock bypass still needs time, access, cover, and consequences if discovered.
- Survival equipment should change the cost of failure, not erase hazards automatically.

## Common Edge Cases

- Some repair and salvage tasks are impossible without appropriate tools; others are possible but penalized. Decide this before the roll.
- Multiple tool kits may help, but do not stack unlimited gear bonuses.
- Quick charging, slow charging, grid dependence, and device capacity all matter when power is scarce.
- Neural interrogation, invasive surveillance, and similar gear can carry legal, moral, and setting consequences even when mechanically available.
- Jump packs, gliders, parachutes, rappelling gear, and climbing systems should still require positioning and skill when failure would matter.
