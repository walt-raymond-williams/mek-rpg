# Optional Personal Combat Rules

## Purpose

Use this summary only when the GM explicitly turns on optional personal-combat rules for extra detail, drama, or reduced lethality. These options can change tone, speed, and danger.

## When to Use

- The table wants morale checks for fear, shock, collapse, or rallying.
- The table wants hit locations and location-specific wounds.
- A character may be knocked down by a wound, melee impact, or environmental hazard.
- The GM wants personal combat to be less lethal by increasing damage capacity or changing how armor converts damage.
- The user specifically asks about optional personal-combat procedures.

## Do Not Use For

- Default personal combat. Use the main `rules/personal-combat/` summaries unless an option is deliberately enabled.
- Kid-friendly play without checking tone and safety expectations first.
- Copying hit-location tables, wound-effect tables, fatigue tables, or worked examples.
- BattleMech, vehicle, aerospace, or lance-scale tactical combat.

## Basic Procedure

1. Before combat, state which optional rule is active: morale checks, hit locations, knockdown checks, increased damage capacity, increased armor effectiveness, or a limited subset.
2. Apply the option to all relevant participants unless the GM has a clear campaign reason not to. Lethality-reduction options should apply to PCs and NPCs consistently.
3. For morale checks, call for a Willpower-based check when shock, fear, friendly losses, reinforcements, terrifying enemies, or other dramatic pressure would plausibly break resolve.
4. If a morale check fails, apply the source demoralized effects and allow later rally attempts through leadership, communication, or major positive events.
5. For hit locations, roll location after a successful hit, adjust for attack direction, apply location-specific armor, then apply the source location damage and wound effects.
6. If hit locations are active, use the source surgery and recovery requirements for specific wound effects.
7. For knockdown checks, call for the source Reflexes-based check when a location effect, strong melee hit, or GM-defined hazard could put the character on the ground.
8. For lethality reduction, choose either increased damage/fatigue capacity or increased armor effectiveness before combat starts. Do not switch mid-fight to save one side.
9. Record which options were active in the session log when the result affects campaign continuity.

## Practical GM Guidance

- Optional rules are not free detail. Morale adds roleplay pressure, hit locations add injury specificity, knockdown adds positional friction, and lethality reduction changes campaign survivability.
- Hit locations slow play and can make combat harsher. Use them for duels, gritty missions, or medically important scenes, not every random exchange.
- Morale checks should follow meaningful events, not every missed shot. Use them to model shock, command, and collapse.
- Knockdown is useful when position matters: catwalks, ice, rubble, melee rushes, leg wounds, or exposed fire lanes.
- Lethality reduction is best as a campaign-level tone choice. Apply it openly and consistently.

## Common Edge Cases

- Morale effects can interact with Combat Sense, Combat Paralysis, command, and Leadership. Check the cited source when those traits matter.
- A demoralized character may need a commander who can communicate to rally during combat.
- Hit locations replace ordinary all-purpose injury penalties for many action and movement effects, but ordinary injury modifiers still matter for consciousness, knockdown, and healing checks.
- Splash and melee interactions with hit locations have special source handling; inspect the page before applying exact effects.
- Knockdown can stun a character without adding standard damage unless the fall itself creates a separate hazard.
- Increased armor effectiveness does not help when an attack's penetration is high enough to ignore the armor.
