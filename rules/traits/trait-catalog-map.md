# Trait Catalog Map

## Purpose

Use this routing aid to locate the right trait source area and decide what kind of sheet question a trait creates. It does not reproduce the trait catalog, trait tables, random unit assignment tables, or exact modifiers.

## When to Use

- A player proposes a positive, negative, flexible, multiple, identity-based, or vehicle trait.
- A sheet review needs to identify missing trait descriptors, trait points, aliases, opposing traits, or vehicle links.
- A GM needs to know whether a trait is mostly personal, social, economic, medical, sensory, training-related, vehicle-related, or campaign-facing.
- A trait question requires the legally owned source page rather than an existing broad character summary.

## Do Not Use For

- Copying the master trait list, trait descriptions, trait tables, vehicle assignment tables, or unit lists.
- Applying exact trait modifiers from memory. Use the cited source pages.
- Treating vehicle traits as tactical unit records. Route unit combat to `gm/switch-to-classic-battletech.md`, MegaMek, or MekHQ.
- Buying, improving, or removing traits after play without `rules/character-creation/xp-advancement.md` and GM approval.

## Basic Procedure

1. Identify the trait's role: character trait, vehicle trait, positive, negative, flexible, multiple, identity-based, opposed, variable-level, restricted, or creation-only.
2. Record trait points and whether the trait is active. A trait whose full XP value has not been reached should be recorded as progress, not applied.
3. Add descriptors for traits that need them: alias, contact, enemy, dependent, obligation, property, rank, reputation, body part, implant, vehicle, vehicle quirk, or other scope.
4. Check for opposing traits or opposing character features before finalizing the sheet.
5. Route the trait to the private source page range by topic cluster.
6. Record the page reference and any unresolved GM ruling on the character sheet or campaign note.
7. For vehicle traits, also update campaign asset notes when the vehicle is an important campaign object.

## Practical GM Guidance

- Translate traits into campaign hooks. Enemies, dependents, rank, title, property, reputation, secrets, obligations, and connections should create scenes.
- For identity-based traits, keep alias ownership explicit. A resource tied to one identity can become a liability if used under another.
- For medical or disability traits, preserve player consent and campaign tone. Record the mechanical need without making the trait a surprise punishment.
- For vehicle traits, separate character ownership/access from unit identity, readiness, tactical record, and MekHQ ownership.
- If a trait points to equipment, implants, prosthetics, drugs, vehicles, property, rank, or money, read the relevant equipment or campaign summary before adjudicating consequences.

## Common Edge Cases

- Multiple traits can repeat if their descriptors differ; do not merge them without source or GM support.
- Flexible traits can be positive or negative. The sign matters for XP and fiction.
- Some negative traits are opposed by skill levels or other character features rather than a named positive trait.
- A vehicle trait can point to table-heavy unit selection. The committed answer should be "inspect the source table" rather than a copied list.
- A revealed social trait, secret, rank issue, enemy action, or vehicle loss may create campaign consequences outside character creation.
