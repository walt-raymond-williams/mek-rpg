# Advancement

## Purpose

Use this summary when play produces XP, character improvement, training opportunities, rank growth, salaries, bonuses, or other advancement consequences.

## When to Use

- A session, mission, campaign step, roleplaying moment, or major achievement is being rewarded.
- A player wants to spend XP after play starts.
- A character trains for a new or improved skill.
- Aging, rank, salary, or career status affects the campaign.
- The GM needs to decide whether a reward is XP, money, reputation, contact access, rank, property, or a future hook.

## Do Not Use For

- Character build math before play. Use `rules/character-creation/overview.md`.
- Exact XP award, XP cost, aging, salary, bonus, or monthly expense table values. Inspect the cited private source pages.
- Injury healing or surgery details. Use `rules/campaign/injuries-recovery.md`.
- Force-level mercenary accounting, BattleMech maintenance, or tactical campaign logistics.

## Basic Procedure

1. Run a short feedback pass after play. Identify group mission performance, larger campaign progress, individual development, roleplaying, major achievements, and table entertainment.
2. Award XP conservatively. Use the private award table when exact values matter, and keep exceptional awards rare enough to remain meaningful.
3. Record earned and unspent XP in `pcs.md` and summarize the award in `session-log.md`.
4. When XP is spent, identify the target: attribute, Edge, trait, skill, or another campaign resource.
5. Require a plausible in-fiction explanation. Training, practice, mentors, field experience, medical recovery, faction support, or downtime can justify the improvement.
6. For skills, check whether training is required and whether a trainer, facility, time, and successful Training check are needed.
7. For traits, require GM approval and a clear event or obligation. Do not treat a negative trait gained during play as a free creation rebate unless the GM intentionally awards XP for that drawback.
8. For rank, salary, bonuses, and expenses, use the private source tables only when the money or authority will affect play.

## Practical GM Guidance

XP is feedback as much as currency. Tell players the broad reasons they advanced, but do not expose hidden campaign scoring in a way that teaches them to chase the table instead of the fiction.

Use non-XP rewards when they fit better: a reliable contact, a repair slot, a letter of passage, temporary shelter, a debt called in later, or a faction reputation shift can matter more than points.

## Common Edge Cases

- Downtime can generate slow advancement, but it competes with recovery, work, repairs, travel, and mission clocks.
- A trainer normally needs to be better than the student in the target skill. Lack of facilities, tools, or time can delay training.
- Salary and bonuses can become bookkeeping-heavy. Use them when employment, mercenary service, or faction support is central to the campaign.
- Rank advancement brings obligations and command responsibility, not just pay and status.
- Aging effects and some trait changes are table-driven; cite the private source instead of restating tables.
