# Campaign Overview

## Purpose

Use this summary as the live-play entry point for consequences that persist after a scene: XP, money, contracts, contacts, reputation, injury recovery, downtime, mission readiness, rank, property, and other obligations.

## When to Use

- A mission or session has ended and the GM needs to update durable consequences.
- A player asks what changed after a job, favor, injury, promotion, payday, or faction reaction.
- The table needs to decide whether the next mission can start now or requires downtime, treatment, repair, training, or negotiation first.
- The GM is saving campaign state after meaningful play.

## Do Not Use For

- Immediate roll resolution. Use `rules/core/`.
- Personal-scale attacks, wounds, stabilization, or surgery details. Use `rules/personal-combat/`.
- Exact XP, salary, bonus, expense, property, rank, reputation, or trait tables. Inspect the cited private source pages.
- Full mercenary force accounting, tactical repairs, contracts, or unit campaign management better handled by MekHQ or another BattleTech campaign tool.

## Basic Procedure

1. Identify the consequence category: advancement, contract aftermath, contact/favor use, reputation change, injury recovery, downtime, mission readiness, wealth/property, or rank/power.
2. Open the focused campaign summary for that category before making the ruling.
3. If the ruling changes a character, update the active campaign folder's `pcs.md`.
4. If it changes NPCs, factions, contacts, promises, grudges, or public standing, update `npcs.md`, `factions.md`, and `relationships.md`.
5. If it changes money, salaries, cargo, medical costs, contracts, salvage, permits, vehicles, or property, update `assets.md`.
6. If it changes objectives, deadlines, readiness, recovery clocks, or future pressure, update `missions.md`, `hooks.md`, and `current-state.md`.
7. Add the session-facing explanation, important rolls, XP, costs, rewards, and unresolved rules gaps to `session-log.md`.

## Practical GM Guidance

Campaign consequences should make choices matter without turning every scene into accounting. Record the fact that matters for future play, cite the rule route if needed, and defer exact table work until a number affects a decision.

Separate rules-supported effects from GM campaign judgment. A salary, bonus, property income, downtime XP, or training roll may have source procedures; a custom contract clause, faction rumor, or employer reaction is usually a campaign-state fact unless a later source provides more detail.

## Common Edge Cases

- Some consequences are both mechanical and narrative. Example: a battlefield injury may impose recovery time, medical obligations, and a visible scar or trait change.
- Rank, title, property, connections, and reputation can change campaign scale. Agree early whether those are foreground play or background color.
- Public reputation and faction-specific reputation can diverge. Record the audience.
- Secret actions may affect hidden NPC knowledge before they affect public standing.
- If a consequence depends on BattleMech repairs, unit readiness, salvage accounting, or tactical damage state, use the tactical handoff or MekHQ-style support instead of inventing RPG-only mechanics.
