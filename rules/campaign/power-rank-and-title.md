# Power Rank And Title

## Purpose

Use this routing aid when military rank, noble title, Clan social status, political authority, wealth, command access, or social power affects a scene or long-term campaign state.

## When to Use

- A PC, NPC, patron, enemy, or contact has a military rank, noble title, Clan status, office, or social authority.
- The GM needs to judge how rank or title changes access, obligation, reaction, command pressure, or legal exposure.
- A character receives promotion, command, property, income, office, or a title as a campaign consequence.
- Clan social status, Bloodname status, or Inner Sphere title equivalence matters to reactions.

## Do Not Use For

- Reproducing title/rank equivalency tables, rank lists, trait recommendations, or social-rank catalogs.
- Treating title equivalence as universal law across all governments, Clans, units, or eras.
- Granting command authority, wealth, or property without recording campaign obligations.
- Resolving exact military organization charts from memory.

## Basic Procedure

1. Identify the authority type:
   - military rank
   - noble title
   - civil office
   - Clan social status
   - property, wealth, or corporate power
   - informal influence
2. Identify the jurisdiction. Rank or title matters differently inside a unit, on a planet, within a realm, among Clans, or in hostile territory.
3. Decide what the authority can actually do in the scene: command troops, request resources, gain access, intimidate, owe duties, draw scrutiny, or create rivals.
4. If exact equivalence, trait effects, or rank/title details matter, inspect the cited source pages.
5. Tie any new status to obligations: superior orders, subordinates, dependents, enemies, audits, public reputation, family claims, or political backlash.
6. Update the active campaign save when power, rank, title, property, income, or command relationships change.

## Practical GM Guidance

- Rank and title are not just rewards. They create responsibility, visibility, and enemies.
- A low-ranking NPC can still be important if they control a gate, roster, file, vehicle key, local custom, or bureaucratic approval.
- High-status encounters should feel consequential. Make the agenda, risk, and possible fallout clear before dice are rolled.
- Clan social rank does not map cleanly to Inner Sphere nobility. Treat cross-culture reactions as a source lookup or table-canon decision.
- If the status affects a PC sheet trait, salary, property, or command role, record the change and source basis.
