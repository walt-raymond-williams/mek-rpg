# Injuries And Recovery

## Purpose

Use this summary when injury, fatigue, medical care, surgery, lasting impairment, treatment access, or recovery time affects ongoing campaign play.

## When to Use

- Combat or an accident has ended and injuries need campaign follow-up.
- A PC needs stabilization, evacuation, normal healing, assisted healing, surgery, or convalescence.
- The next mission may start before a character is fully healed.
- Medical costs, favors, clinic access, prosthetics, implants, or debt matter.
- A lasting wound may become a trait, attribute penalty, scar, obligation, or story hook.

## Do Not Use For

- Immediate damage application during an attack. Use `rules/personal-combat/damage.md`.
- Exact surgery success/failure table values, medical item stats, or trait point values. Inspect the cited private source pages.
- Tactical BattleTech pilot damage or unit repair.
- Killing or permanently maiming a child player character without explicit adult approval.

## Basic Procedure

1. Start from the personal-combat recovery summary. Confirm current standard damage, fatigue, bleeding, stun, specific wounds, and whether stabilization has occurred.
2. Decide whether normal rest is enough or whether assisted healing/surgery is required. Severe trauma and specific surgical wounds require source review.
3. Identify treatment access: field aid, medkit, clinic, hospital, specialist, transport, faction doctor, or contact favor.
4. Record the recovery state in `pcs.md`: current damage/fatigue, injury modifiers if tracked, treatment status, required care, recovery clock, and any activity limits.
5. If treatment creates cost or obligation, update `assets.md`, `contacts.md`-style NPC notes in `npcs.md`, and `relationships.md`.
6. If the injury changes mission timing, update `missions.md`, `hooks.md`, and `current-state.md`.
7. If surgery or poor recovery creates lasting effects, record the trait/attribute/story consequence only after checking the private source and getting any required table consent.

## Practical GM Guidance

Injury recovery should create a clear decision: rest, pay for better care, call in a favor, launch wounded, send a substitute, or delay and risk the mission clock. Give the player the consequence in plain language before asking for that choice.

Keep severe injury narration table-appropriate. In kid-friendly mode, preserve stakes with pain, fear, repairs, rescue, and downtime pressure without dwelling on graphic detail.

## Common Edge Cases

- Fatigue often clears much faster than standard damage; do not make it block downtime unless the source procedure says it does.
- Strenuous activity can slow or interrupt healing.
- Assisted healing depends on facilities and regular care. Losing either can downgrade recovery.
- Surgery is limited by time, equipment, facility quality, the patient's condition, and the surgeon's skill.
- Corrective surgery can improve a bad result but carries risk.
- A character may be medically stable but still not mission-ready.
- Prosthetics, implants, and major replacements are equipment/trait consequences and need source lookup before finalizing.
