# Contacts

## Purpose

Use this summary when an NPC, organization, connection, patron, subordinate, ally, enemy, or owed favor affects what the PCs can learn, borrow, buy, hide, access, or avoid.

## When to Use

- A PC invokes a contact, connection, rank, title, faction tie, property staff, or past favor.
- The GM needs to decide whether a contact can provide information, money, gear, personnel, shelter, introductions, or leverage.
- A contact asks for payment, secrecy, protection, repayment, or a future favor.
- A failed attempt to reach a contact may alert enemies or create a complication.

## Do Not Use For

- Generic social checks with no durable relationship. Use `rules/core/skill-checks.md`.
- Public or faction standing that is not tied to a specific person or network. Use `rules/campaign/reputation.md`.
- Exact Connections, Enemy, In For Life, Property, Rank, Title, Wealth, or Reputation trait tables. Inspect the cited private source pages.
- Inventing a contact that contradicts the active campaign save.

## Basic Procedure

1. Identify the relationship source: a recorded NPC, a campaign promise, a trait such as Connections/Rank/Property/Title, an employer, an old enemy, or a newly earned favor.
2. Define the ask in practical terms: information, funds, equipment, manpower, access, protection, medical help, training, or an introduction.
3. Check the contact's scope. Record region, faction, identity/alias, available communication, and whether the current location makes contact plausible.
4. Choose the appropriate social or professional skill route. Underworld, military, political, corporate, and local-community contacts may use different skills.
5. On success, provide aid that fits the contact's resources and motive. Avoid giving broad permanent assets unless the trait or campaign state supports it.
6. On failure, withhold aid or attach a cost. Serious failure can expose the request to an enemy or create a new hook.
7. Record favors owed, changed attitudes, debts, promises, and future risks in `npcs.md`, `relationships.md`, `factions.md`, and `hooks.md`.

## Practical GM Guidance

Contacts work best when they have limits. Give them a name, an agenda, a location or channel, a reason to help, and something they will not do. A contact should make the next scene sharper, not solve the whole mission off-screen.

Use contact pressure both ways. A player can ask a retired sergeant for a supply lead; that sergeant can later ask the PC to protect a nephew, repay a loan, or keep a scandal quiet.

## Common Edge Cases

- Identity-based traits belong to a specific alias. A contact for one identity may not help another.
- Regional connections may fail in isolation, while rank or reputation may still influence locals if they recognize it.
- A contact can become a patron, dependent, rival, or antagonist. Move them from a passing note into `npcs.md` when they recur.
- Borrowed gear or money is not free property. Record return terms, debt, and consequences for loss.
- High-rank or high-title contacts can shift campaign scale; consider whether the scene should stay personal or move to strategic handoff.
