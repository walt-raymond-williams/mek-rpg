# Contracts

## Purpose

Use this summary to record mission agreements, employment terms, payment, salvage expectations, obligations, breaches, and aftermath in the active campaign save.

## When to Use

- The PCs accept, negotiate, complete, fail, abandon, or breach a job.
- Payment, salary, bonus, salvage, supplies, transport, medical care, legal cover, permits, or debt matters.
- An employer, patron, faction, contact, or mercenary client changes attitude because of mission performance.
- The GM needs to save what was promised and what remains unresolved.

## Do Not Use For

- Full BattleTech mercenary contract construction, force accounting, repair budgets, or strategic logistics. Use MekHQ or a BattleTech campaign tool when that detail matters.
- Exact salary, bonus, expense, gear, or acquisition table values. Inspect the cited private source pages.
- Social negotiation roll details. Use `rules/core/skill-checks.md` and the relevant skill summary.
- Inventing salvage or ownership rules not present in the current campaign save or source summaries.

## Basic Procedure

1. Define the contracting parties: employer, intermediary, faction, unit, guarantor, and any hidden principal if known to the GM.
2. Record the objective, constraints, deadline, expected opposition, legal status, and what counts as success, partial success, failure, or breach.
3. Record compensation categories: cash, salary, bonus, salvage, gear, transport, medical treatment, repairs, contacts, permits, reputation, rank support, or future favors.
4. During negotiation, use core skill checks and relevant traits/contacts/reputation. Do not create exact economic values without checking the source or campaign state.
5. At mission close, compare outcome to terms. Decide payment, bonus, withheld support, debt, employer reaction, faction reaction, and unresolved clauses.
6. Update `missions.md` for objective status, `assets.md` for money/salvage/debts/repairs, `factions.md` for posture, `relationships.md` for trust or grudges, and `hooks.md` for follow-up pressure.
7. If the contract creates unit-scale bookkeeping, record the gap and route tactical or logistics detail to external support.

## Practical GM Guidance

Contracts should create choices, not just payouts. Good terms can force tradeoffs between money, time, loyalty, collateral damage, salvage, secrecy, political cost, and future access.

The searched A Time of War campaign pages support rewards, salaries, bonuses, wealth/property, rank, contacts, and reputation, but they do not appear to provide a dedicated mercenary-contract subsystem in the reviewed range. Treat contract structure as GM campaign-state procedure unless a later source is added.

## Common Edge Cases

- Hidden employers can change aftermath without changing what the PCs were openly promised.
- Salvage and vehicle ownership can cross into tactical BattleTech or MekHQ-style records.
- A failed mission can still pay partially, earn respect, create a contact, or reveal information.
- A successful mission can still damage reputation if collateral damage, betrayal, or faction politics matter.
- Non-cash rewards should be saved explicitly so they are usable later.
