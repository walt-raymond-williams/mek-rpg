# Downtime And Mission Readiness

## Purpose

Use this summary when time passes between missions and the GM needs to determine what the characters can recover, train, earn, repair, prepare, or risk before the next operation.

## When to Use

- The campaign has days, weeks, or months between scenes or missions.
- A player wants to train, work, heal, maintain contacts, shop, repair gear, research, travel, or prepare.
- Injuries, fatigue, medical care, ammunition, gear, vehicles, money, legal status, morale, or faction pressure may block the next mission.
- The GM needs a readiness check before launching a dangerous scene.

## Do Not Use For

- Detailed treatment, surgery, or wound recovery. Use `rules/campaign/injuries-recovery.md`.
- Exact downtime XP, salary, expense, equipment, repair, or training table values. Inspect the cited private source pages.
- BattleMech or vehicle maintenance that needs tactical-system detail.
- Skipping consequences just to reach the next action scene.

## Basic Procedure

1. Define the available time and pressure: immediate follow-up, overnight rest, several days, weeks of transit, or a long campaign gap.
2. Ask each important PC or campaign asset for one primary downtime focus: heal, train, work, repair, acquire gear, cultivate contacts, investigate, travel, lay low, or prepare.
3. Apply the focused rule route:
   - Healing or surgery: `rules/campaign/injuries-recovery.md`.
   - XP, training, or downtime advancement: `rules/campaign/advancement.md`.
   - Gear acquisition or repair: `rules/equipment/overview.md`.
   - Contacts or favors: `rules/campaign/contacts.md`.
   - Reputation or faction fallout: `rules/campaign/reputation.md`.
   - Contract aftermath or new work: `rules/campaign/contracts.md`.
4. Check mission readiness before the next operation: PC condition, treatment status, key gear, ammunition, armor, transport, money, permits, contacts, faction heat, deadlines, and tactical handoff needs.
5. Record what changed in the active campaign folder. Use `pcs.md`, `assets.md`, `missions.md`, `hooks.md`, `relationships.md`, and `current-state.md` as the main owners.
6. If the table launches despite a readiness gap, state the risk clearly and apply it as a scene pressure.

## Practical GM Guidance

Downtime is not empty time. It is where consequences become visible: the wound still aches, the employer delays payment, a tech needs parts, a rival spreads rumors, or a trainer demands a favor.

Keep the workflow compact. For each downtime interval, record the interval, each PC's focus, any rolls made, what improved, what got worse, and what remains unresolved.

## Common Edge Cases

- Downtime XP is slow and may depend on Intelligence, learner traits, career skill use, and available time.
- Training can be interrupted without necessarily losing all progress, but the check normally waits until the required time has passed.
- A character can be healed enough to act but not enough to operate without penalties or risk.
- Travel downtime may not support all careers or training options.
- A mission deadline may force the group to choose between readiness and opportunity.
- Readiness for an RPG-scale scene is not the same as readiness for a tactical BattleTech engagement.
