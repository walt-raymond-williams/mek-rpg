# Tactical Damage And Traits

## Purpose

Use this routing aid when a tactical addendum question concerns damage conversion between vehicle-scale and character-scale combat, vehicular weapon traits, battle armor weapons, pilot or crew injury, physical attacks, stealth systems, or tactical end-phase effects.

## When to Use

- Vehicle-scale weapons, battle armor weapons, or physical attacks can harm infantry, dismounted characters, or named PCs.
- Personal weapons, support weapons, or cohesive infantry fire are being used against vehicles.
- A player asks how weapon traits such as anti-infantry, area effect, missiles, pulse, rapid fire, or heat-causing weapons change tactical handling.
- A tactical result causes pilot, MechWarrior, or crew damage that must return to the RPG character sheet.
- The GM needs to know whether stealth equipment, continuous damage, fatigue, consciousness, or vehicle end-phase issues affect the scene.

## Do Not Use For

- Reproducing anti-infantry, missile, battle armor weapon, melee modifier, pilot damage, or stealth equipment tables.
- Exact damage math, hit locations, armor location, critical hits, cluster handling, or weapon trait effects from memory.
- Deciding full tactical outcomes without Total Warfare, Tactical Operations, MegaMek, MekHQ, or private source lookup.
- Replacing personal combat wound, armor, barrier, or healing summaries after the tactical result is known.

## Basic Procedure

1. Identify the damage bridge:
   - personal weapons against vehicles
   - cohesive infantry fire against vehicles
   - vehicle weapons against conventional infantry or dismounted characters
   - vehicle weapons against battle armor
   - battle armor weapons or melee against ordinary infantry
   - tactical event damage to pilots or crew
2. If exact survival, armor, location, or weapon trait behavior matters, route to the private source or tactical tool before resolving.
3. Preserve the original tactical damage and the RPG-facing consequence in the handoff notes.
4. For vehicle weapons against infantry, distinguish direct hits, nearby strike effects, area effects, and missed shots that still scatter or affect space.
5. For battle armor, identify whether the attack is handled as vehicle-scale combat or personal combat before choosing skills and damage rules.
6. For pilot or crew damage, apply the tactical result as a bridge back to A Time of War damage, then update wounds, recovery, and campaign state through the appropriate summaries.
7. In the End Phase, handle continuous personal effects through personal combat summaries and vehicle-specific effects through the tactical source/tool.

## Practical GM Guidance

- This is the highest-risk part of the tactical bridge for PCs. Vehicle-scale weapons are often overwhelming to ordinary characters.
- Keep the distinction between AP/BAR, BD, tactical armor, personal armor, and Total Warfare armor explicit in notes. Do not collapse them into a single "damage" number.
- Weapon traits are not flavor text. They can change area, timing, rate, target handling, or damage shape, so exact cases require lookup.
- Battle armor weapons are a special middle layer. Many suit-mounted weapons use personal combat handling against ordinary infantry while retaining tactical behavior against vehicles.
- Pilot and crew injury should not be lost when the fight leaves the tactical tool. Bring it back into `pcs.md`, `npcs.md`, `assets.md`, and recovery notes.

## Common Edge Cases

- Margin of Success and Margin of Failure can still affect personal-scale damage and missed shots in tactical combat; exact application belongs to the source.
- Anti-infantry and rapid-fire weapons may operate on the shorter tactical turn differently from standard heavy weapons.
- Area-effect weapons can affect friendly and hostile characters in the affected space; mapless handling needs source support.
- Battle armor melee depends on suit features and carried or mounted weapons. Do not summarize table entries as a generic bonus.
- Stealth equipment should be routed through existing stealth gear concepts, but vehicle size and system type matter.
