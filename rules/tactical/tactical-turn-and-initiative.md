# Tactical Turn And Initiative

## Purpose

Use this routing aid when a tactical addendum question is about turn length, initiative, movement order, action sequence, simultaneous resolution, or out-of-contact command effects.

## When to Use

- A scene has tactical units and the GM needs the A Time of War tactical turn structure.
- A player asks who rolls initiative for a force, squad, or vehicle group.
- Communications, ECM, command separation, Combat Sense, Combat Paralysis, Leadership, or Tactics affect tactical initiative.
- The GM needs to explain why tactical damage can still occur simultaneously even after a unit is destroyed or disabled.

## Do Not Use For

- Personal combat initiative with individual character turns. Use `rules/personal-combat/initiative.md`.
- Full BattleTech initiative rules or unit movement ratios from memory. Use Total Warfare, MegaMek, MekHQ, or the private source when exact handling matters.
- Reproducing the tactical skills or vehicle action complexity tables.
- Resolving a large tactical scenario inside MEK-RPG.

## Basic Procedure

1. Confirm that the scene is using tactical addendum timing. A tactical combat turn represents a shorter RPG-compatible span than a standard Total Warfare turn.
2. Use the tactical sequence:
   - Initiative Phase
   - Action Phase
   - End Phase
3. For tactical initiative, identify one commander per side or force when possible.
4. Apply only recorded Leadership, Tactics, Combat Sense, Combat Paralysis, and communication-state effects that the source supports.
5. If force members are out of contact, decide whether the optional out-of-contact rule is enabled before initiative is rolled.
6. Move units in initiative order using tactical-system proportional movement logic. Do not guess exact Total Warfare movement-ratio handling from memory.
7. Resolve attacks and other effects with simultaneous-resolution expectations: a unit disabled during the turn may still have actions whose effects occur before the turn ends.

## Practical GM Guidance

- Tactical initiative is force-oriented, not a personal-combat initiative roll for every named PC.
- The commander matters. If a PC is commanding, record their Leadership, Tactics, relevant traits, communications status, and which units are outside command contact.
- If the players want exact initiative timing, movement order, or unit-by-unit activation, move to the tactical handoff.
- Treat RPG advantages such as ambush, recon, or command prep as setup facts first. Do not invent tactical modifiers when a better handoff packet can preserve the advantage.
- Combat Paralysis can affect commanders and unit controllers differently. Verify the exact scope in source before applying it to a crew member who is not actually controlling the unit.

## Common Edge Cases

- Infantry squads may act as one tactical unit even when named characters are inside the squad.
- Out-of-contact effects are optional and should not appear by surprise after players chose their deployment.
- Ties, commander separation, and trait interactions can be tactically meaningful; route exact edge cases to the source or tactical tool.
- Tactical action resolution differs from personal combat because movement and combat are in one action phase while effects still resolve together.
- A player may ask to "hold" or "pre-empt" based on a special ability. Check `rules/tactical/heat-and-pilot-abilities.md` and the source before granting it.
