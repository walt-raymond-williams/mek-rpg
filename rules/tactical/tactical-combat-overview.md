# Tactical Combat Overview

## Purpose

Use this routing aid when an RPG scene touches the A Time of War tactical combat addendum and the GM needs to decide whether to stay in RPG mode, use the addendum as a bridge, or hand the encounter to Classic BattleTech, MegaMek, or MekHQ.

## When to Use

- A scene includes vehicles, BattleMechs, battle armor, ProtoMechs, aerospace units, spacecraft, turrets, or infantry squads on a shared battlefield.
- The GM needs the distinction between infantry units, vehicular units, and battle armor before choosing a rules route.
- A player asks how A Time of War characters relate to Total Warfare-style tactical units.
- The GM needs skill conversion, crew-role, or map-scale reminders before preparing a tactical handoff packet.

## Do Not Use For

- Running full tactical BattleTech combat from MEK-RPG summaries.
- Reproducing tactical skill tables, unit records, squad sheets, weapon tables, or modifier lists.
- Exact hex movement, facing, armor locations, heat, ammunition, critical hits, or weapon ranges. Use Classic BattleTech, MegaMek, MekHQ, Total Warfare, Tactical Operations, or the private source.
- Personal-scale fights with no vehicle-scale units. Use `rules/personal-combat/`.

## Basic Procedure

1. Identify the battle scale:
   - personal combat between dismounted characters or infantry
   - RPG-scale vehicle scene with one decisive risk
   - small tactical bridge where A Time of War characters operate vehicle-scale units
   - full tactical combat owned by Classic BattleTech, MegaMek, or MekHQ
2. Classify each important participant as infantry, battle armor, or vehicular unit.
3. Treat battle armor as a boundary case:
   - against vehicles or other battle armor, it usually uses vehicle-scale tactical handling.
   - against conventional infantry or dismounted characters, personal combat handling is often the better route.
4. If tactical play continues inside A Time of War, prepare a consistent map or terrain scale and track unit positions, facings, and footprints.
5. Identify the relevant warrior or crew role for each unit: commander, driver, pilot, gunner, artillery user, squad leader, or individual trooper.
6. Convert only the skill information needed for the handoff. Keep RPG skills, tactical ratings, assumptions, traits, and missing data visible in the encounter notes.
7. If the scene grows beyond a small tactical exchange or if exact unit survival matters, stop and use the external tactical system.

## Practical GM Guidance

- The tactical addendum is explicitly advanced and can slow play. It is most useful when the table wants a bridge for a small number of units, not when a full battle should be resolved quickly and faithfully.
- The addendum tries to preserve compatibility with Total Warfare while keeping A Time of War characters on the field. When systems conflict, decide which rules authority governs before dice are rolled.
- Dismounted characters are vulnerable around vehicle weapons. Do not blur the scale boundary when PC survival is at stake.
- Use the tactical addendum to prepare handoff facts: who commands, who drives, who fires, what skills exist, what unit state matters, and what must return to campaign files.
- For infantry squads and battle armor squads, decide whether cohesion is being used before handling actions. Cohesion changes who rolls and how the squad is represented.

## Common Edge Cases

- Total Warfare modifiers are target-number modifiers there, but A Time of War applies modifiers to the roll result. Convert carefully and cite the source if exact modifiers matter.
- If a tactical unit has known Total Warfare ratings but no RPG attributes or traits, record temporary assumptions rather than treating the pilot as fully built.
- Personal hit locations are discouraged at tactical scale unless a named character's injury location matters after the fight.
- Squad record sheets and tactical record sheets are source/back-matter lookups. Do not recreate them in summaries.
- A "small" tactical encounter can still be too complex if it has many infantry, aircraft, terrain interactions, or special equipment.
