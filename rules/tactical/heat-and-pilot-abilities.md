# Heat And Pilot Abilities

## Purpose

Use this routing aid when a tactical addendum question concerns heat for BattleMechs or fighters, Special Pilot Abilities, advanced Gunnery/Piloting options, or tactical abilities that affect initiative, movement, attacks, heat, or morale.

## When to Use

- A BattleMech or aerospace fighter builds heat during a tactical bridge.
- Heat could affect movement, weapon attacks, shutdown, ammunition, pilot damage, or random movement.
- A PC or NPC wants to acquire, use, verify, or route a Special Pilot Ability.
- A tactical encounter needs to record advanced Gunnery or Piloting ability assumptions before export to Classic BattleTech, MegaMek, or MekHQ.

## Do Not Use For

- Reproducing the Special Pilot Ability catalog, prerequisites, XP costs, or exact ability text.
- Running heat accounting, shutdown, ammunition explosion, or movement penalties from summary memory.
- Granting abilities during character creation without source verification and GM approval.
- Treating a high Piloting or Gunnery skill as automatic permission to use an advanced ability.

## Basic Procedure

1. If heat matters, decide whether the scene should remain in A Time of War tactical bridge mode or move to a tactical tool.
2. Record the unit, heat-producing actions, existing heat, and any external heat sources for the tactical handoff.
3. Remember that tactical addendum heat uses the shorter A Time of War turn scale; exact heat dissipation and consequences require source or tool handling.
4. For Special Pilot Abilities, verify:
   - ability class
   - linked skill or field
   - minimum skill level
   - prerequisites
   - XP cost
   - class maximums
   - GM approval based on demonstrated style and training
5. When an ability modifies tactical movement, attacks, initiative, physical combat, heat, or morale, route exact effects to the source or tactical tool.
6. Record approved abilities on the PC/NPC sheet and tactical handoff packet, including unresolved source checks.

## Practical GM Guidance

- Heat is a tactical-state system. If the players care about exact heat, use MegaMek, MekHQ, tabletop rules, or the source instead of approximating.
- Special Pilot Abilities are closer to earned tactical traits than normal skills. They should grow out of play, training, and source-backed prerequisites.
- The addendum groups abilities by broad use, including Gunnery, Piloting, and miscellaneous abilities. Class limits matter for long-term character development.
- Some abilities modify ordinary tactical procedures rather than creating standalone actions. Read the exact source entry before applying one in play.
- If an ability expresses a character's style but its mechanics are not yet verified, record the concept as a candidate and mark it `Needs source review`.

## Common Edge Cases

- A low-heat pilot concept should not automatically qualify for a heat-focused ability just because the XP exists.
- Some abilities apply only to specific platforms such as BattleMechs, ProtoMechs, fighters, vehicles, or crewed units.
- Some abilities interact with Tactical Operations or Total Warfare rules. Use the external tactical source/tool for exact interactions.
- Commander-level abilities may affect force initiative or out-of-contact handling; check `rules/tactical/tactical-turn-and-initiative.md` and the source.
- If heat or an ability causes pilot injury, bring the result back through `rules/tactical/tactical-damage-and-traits.md`, personal combat damage, and campaign recovery files.
