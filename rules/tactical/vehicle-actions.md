# Vehicle Actions

## Purpose

Use this routing aid when a tactical addendum question concerns vehicle action complexity, movement conversion, unit movement sequence, jumping, stacking, unit footprint, vehicular attacks, range conversion, or vehicle weapons attacking infantry.

## When to Use

- A BattleMech, vehicle, battle armor suit, ProtoMech, aerospace unit, or other tactical unit moves or attacks in a small A Time of War tactical bridge.
- The GM needs to decide whether a movement or attack is simple enough for RPG resolution or needs a tactical handoff.
- A player asks how meters, Total Warfare movement points, jump movement, unit size, or vehicle footprints interact with A Time of War scenes.
- A vehicle-scale weapon is being aimed at dismounted characters or infantry and survival depends on exact handling.

## Do Not Use For

- Reproducing movement-cost tables, action-complexity tables, unit-size tables, or detailed modifier lists.
- Running exact hex movement, turn modes, aerospace movement, facing, line of sight, physical attack placement, or terrain costs from summary memory.
- Full vehicle-versus-vehicle combat. Use Classic BattleTech, MegaMek, MekHQ, Total Warfare, or Tactical Operations.
- Personal attacks with carried weapons by dismounted characters. Use `rules/personal-combat/` and `rules/equipment/weapons.md`.

## Basic Procedure

1. Confirm the unit type and the movement mode: ground, rail, naval, airborne, aerospace, jump-capable, battle armor, infantry, or dismounted character.
2. Decide the scene route:
   - one meaningful RPG risk
   - small tactical bridge using source at the table
   - external tactical tool for exact combat
3. If staying in tactical addendum mode, convert Total Warfare movement to meters only as the source supports and record the original tactical values.
4. Use the source's unit movement sequence and action-complexity guidance rather than inventing timing.
5. For jumping, identify whether the unit is infantry/battle armor or a non-infantry vehicle-scale unit, because timing and landing handling differ.
6. For stacking and space, use unit footprint and size as a handoff fact. Do not allow large units to share space unless a source-supported exception applies.
7. For vehicular attacks, decide whether the target is another vehicle-scale unit, battle armor, an infantry formation, or an individual character.
8. If an attack can injure a named PC, route exact range, attack modifiers, strike zone, scatter, and damage conversion to source or tactical tooling.

## Practical GM Guidance

- A Time of War tracks tactical movement in meters, while Total Warfare uses hexes and movement points. Preserve both notations in encounter prep when the scene will leave RPG mode.
- Vehicle weapons generally behave like tactical weapons against other vehicle-scale targets. Their interaction with ordinary characters is the dangerous bridge area.
- Battle armor again sits between scales. Against ordinary infantry, personal combat procedures are often the intended route; against vehicles, tactical handling usually applies.
- Do not use the addendum as a shopping list of tactical options. Ask what the unit is trying to accomplish and resolve only the risk that matters.
- For a chase, extraction, hot landing, or single overwatch shot, one RPG skill check plus a stated consequence is often better than partial tactical simulation.

## Common Edge Cases

- Aerospace units can cross ordinary ground-scale battle areas extremely quickly; if that matters, use the source or a tactical tool.
- Turn modes, detailed terrain, building movement, and special movement options point to Tactical Operations or external tools.
- Vehicle weapons may have timing limits across the shorter tactical turns. Track weapon availability only if it matters enough to justify tactical handling.
- Infantry cohesion affects movement and attacks; choose cohesion before resolving movement or fire.
- Missed vehicle fire against infantry may still matter because the source includes scatter and area effects. Do not handwave misses near named PCs.
