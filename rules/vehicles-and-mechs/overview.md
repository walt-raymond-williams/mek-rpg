# Vehicles and BattleMechs Overview

## Purpose

Use this summary to decide how RPG characters interact with vehicles, BattleMechs, battle armor, aerospace craft, and other tactical units without turning the rules assistant into a full tactical BattleTech engine.

## When to Use

- A character drives, pilots, rides in, commands, repairs around, buys, or records a vehicle-scale asset.
- The GM needs to decide whether a scene is an RPG-scale vehicle moment, an A Time of War tactical-combat bridge, or a Classic BattleTech, MegaMek, or MekHQ encounter.
- A character's Driving, Piloting, Gunnery, Sensor Operations, Artillery, Tactics, or command role matters to a vehicle scene.
- The campaign needs to record vehicle ownership, condition, fuel, crew, or tactical handoff notes in `assets.md`.

## Do Not Use For

- Full hex-scale BattleTech combat with facing, heat, weapon range brackets, armor locations, initiative by unit, ammunition, or detailed damage. Use `gm/switch-to-classic-battletech.md`.
- Exact vehicle catalogs, item stat blocks, personal-vehicle table values, fuel prices, or unit records. Inspect the cited private source pages or use the appropriate BattleTech tool.
- Personal-scale firefights between dismounted characters. Use `rules/personal-combat/`.
- Personal gear except where a vehicle is being bought, fueled, operated, or recorded as an asset.

## Basic Procedure

1. Identify the scene scale:
   - routine travel or ownership bookkeeping
   - risky RPG-scale vehicle action
   - A Time of War tactical bridge with a few units and character skill checks
   - full Classic BattleTech, MegaMek, or MekHQ tactical play
2. Identify the vehicle or unit category: personal vehicle, support vehicle, combat vehicle, battle armor, BattleMech, IndustrialMech, ProtoMech, aerospace unit, spacecraft, naval craft, rail vehicle, or fixed turret.
3. Pick the controlling skill:
   - ordinary ground, rail, or sea surface operation usually starts with Driving and the correct subskill.
   - BattleMechs, battle armor, aerospace units, ProtoMechs, spacecraft, and similar combat platforms usually start with Piloting and the correct subskill.
   - mounted vehicle weapons use Gunnery and the correct subskill unless another specific skill, such as Artillery or Support Weapons, clearly applies.
4. If the scene stays in RPG mode, resolve only the meaningful risk: losing control, arriving late, avoiding pursuit, making a difficult maneuver, firing a decisive shot, or protecting passengers.
5. If tactical detail matters, stop and use the tactical handoff. Record the characters' relevant RPG skills and any known converted tactical ratings instead of resolving the battle from summary memory.
6. After the scene, update campaign assets with vehicle condition, location, fuel or maintenance constraints, crew assignments, legal status, damage, salvage, and unresolved source lookups.

## Practical GM Guidance

- Do not roll for safe routine operation. The skill chapter explicitly treats ordinary operation as no-roll unless failure is meaningful.
- Ask what the player wants the vehicle to accomplish. A getaway, stunt, chase, overwatch shot, hot landing, vehicle purchase, and battlefield duel are different scenes.
- Personal vehicles are campaign assets, not just shopping entries. Record ownership, passengers/cargo role, local legality, fuel dependence, and whether the asset is disposable or mission-critical.
- Battle armor sits between personal and vehicle play. Use vehicular rules against other vehicle-scale targets, but personal combat is often the better fit when battle armor interacts with ordinary infantry.
- Keep tactical tool setup small and useful: pilot/gunnery notes, unit identities, battlefield, objectives, withdrawal conditions, and post-battle campaign consequences.

## Common Edge Cases

- Driving and Piloting are not interchangeable. Ground vehicles and rail/sea vehicles point to Driving; BattleMechs, battle armor, aerospace units, ProtoMechs, and spacecraft point to Piloting.
- Gunnery subskills are platform-specific. A good BattleMech gunner is not automatically trained for every vehicle weapon interface.
- Total Warfare modifiers invert when used as A Time of War roll modifiers. Do not apply them from memory; use the cited source or tactical tool.
- The tactical addendum is explicitly advanced and can slow play. For large battles or when precise BattleTech outcomes matter, use the external tactical system directly.
- Vehicle Trait and Custom Vehicle Trait material affects starting access and customization, but exact tables and random assignments remain source lookups.
