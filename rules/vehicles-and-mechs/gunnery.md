# Gunnery

## Purpose

Use this summary when a character fires weapons mounted on BattleMechs, IndustrialMechs, combat vehicles, support vehicles, battle armor, aerospace craft, spacecraft, turrets, or similar vehicle-scale platforms.

## When to Use

- A character fires a mounted weapon from a vehicle, 'Mech, aerospace unit, battle armor suit, fixed turret, or spacecraft.
- A tactical encounter needs RPG character Gunnery skills recorded for unit operation or conversion.
- A scene needs a single decisive mounted-weapon shot without resolving a full tactical battle.
- The GM must decide whether a battle armor weapon, support weapon, small arm, artillery system, or vehicle weapon uses Gunnery or another skill.

## Do Not Use For

- Personal small arms, thrown weapons, melee, or ordinary support weapons carried by a dismounted character. Use `rules/personal-combat/` and `rules/equipment/weapons.md`.
- Artillery tasks where the Artillery skill is the better match.
- Full vehicle-versus-vehicle fire, hit location, armor location, heat, range-bracket, ammunition, or weapon-trait handling. Use the tactical handoff.
- Exact weapon stats, battle armor weapon conversion, rate of fire, anti-infantry weapon traits, or damage conversion tables.

## Basic Procedure

1. Identify the mounted weapon platform and choose the Gunnery subskill: Aerospace, Air Vehicle, Battlesuit, Ground Vehicle, 'Mech, ProtoMech, Sea Vehicle, Spacecraft, or Turret.
2. Confirm whether the weapon is actually mounted. Weapons carried in battle armor manipulators may use Small Arms or Support Weapons instead of Gunnery/Battlesuit.
3. Decide whether the attack stays in RPG mode:
   - use RPG mode for a single dramatic shot, suppression beat, intimidation, or simple consequence.
   - hand off when range, heat, arcs, hit locations, armor, ammunition, multiple units, or weapon timing matter.
4. If staying in RPG mode, resolve the attack as a skill check using the appropriate Gunnery subskill and source-supported modifiers.
5. If drawing on Total Warfare modifiers, invert them as A Time of War roll modifiers rather than target-number modifiers.
6. Resolve only the table-facing consequence needed for the scene: hit or miss, damaged cover, forced withdrawal, passenger risk, alarm, collateral damage, or tactical encounter trigger.
7. Record ammunition, heat, visible signature, legal consequences, damage, or repair needs only if they will matter later.

## Practical GM Guidance

- Gunnery is platform-specific. Treat `Gunnery/'Mech`, `Gunnery/Ground Vehicle`, and `Gunnery/Battlesuit` as different sheet entries.
- A good RPG summary can identify the right skill and handoff point; it should not recreate the weapon tables.
- Vehicle weapons against dismounted characters can be brutally decisive. Use the tactical handoff when survival, cover, and exact damage matter.
- For crewed vehicles, identify who is firing which weapon. The tactical addendum distinguishes commanders, drivers, gunners, and other action users.
- Special Gunnery abilities exist, but they are advanced character development and tactical-facing options. Do not improvise their benefits.

## Common Edge Cases

- Battle armor has mixed scale. Mounted suit weapons can use Gunnery/Battlesuit, while handheld personal weapons may use personal-combat skills.
- Turret weapons use their own Gunnery subskill, including fixed defenses and structures.
- Multi-weapon vehicle attacks, rapid fire, anti-infantry traits, battle armor weapon conversion, and damage footprints are tactical addendum material, not live-rule summary material.
- Gunnery Special Pilot Abilities have prerequisites, XP costs, class limits, and GM approval requirements; cite the source instead of summarizing exact entries during lookup.
- Aerospace, spacecraft, and artillery engagements often depend on scale and external rules. Prefer MegaMek, MekHQ, Total Warfare, Tactical Operations, or Strategic Operations where appropriate.
