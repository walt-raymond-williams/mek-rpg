# MechWarrior Skills

## Purpose

Use this summary to connect an RPG character's sheet to BattleMech, IndustrialMech, battle armor, combat vehicle, aerospace, or tactical crew competence.

## When to Use

- A PC or NPC needs MechWarrior, vehicle crew, fighter pilot, battlesuit, or tactical gunner notes.
- A character sheet needs the correct Piloting or Gunnery subskills recorded before a mission.
- A tactical encounter needs pilot quality, gunner quality, commander roles, crew roles, or conversion notes.
- A player asks which RPG skills matter for driving, piloting, firing, commanding, sensing, or supporting vehicle-scale combat.

## Do Not Use For

- Building a full BattleTech unit record or replacing Total Warfare pilot cards.
- Exact skill-field, academy, Life Module, trait, or XP purchase tables.
- Full tactical combat or detailed vehicle maintenance.
- Treating "MechWarrior" as one universal skill. A Time of War uses specific skills and subskills.

## Basic Procedure

1. Identify the battlefield role: pilot/driver, gunner, commander, artillery operator, sensor operator, technician, tactician, navigator, or passenger.
2. Record the RPG skills that support that role:
   - Piloting subskills for BattleMechs, battle armor, aerospace, ProtoMechs, spacecraft, and similar platforms.
   - Driving subskills for ordinary ground, rail, and sea vehicle operation.
   - Gunnery subskills for mounted weapons by platform.
   - Artillery for artillery systems when the weapon or role calls for it.
   - Sensor Operations, Tactics, Leadership, Technician, Navigation, Communications, or Strategy when the scene is about support, command, repair, route, or coordination.
3. Note linked attributes, target numbers, complexity, subskills, and specialties from the private source or the existing character summary.
4. If converting to tactical play, use `rules/vehicles-and-mechs/converting-to-classic-battletech.md` and record both RPG skill levels and tactical ratings.
5. If assigning Special Pilot Abilities, verify prerequisites, XP cost, skill class limits, demonstrated style, and GM approval from the source pages.
6. Record open gaps directly on the character or asset note rather than silently inventing missing ratings.

## Practical GM Guidance

- Ask for the job, not the title. "I am the MechWarrior" may require Piloting/'Mech, Gunnery/'Mech, Tactics, Sensor Operations, Technician, or command skills depending on the scene.
- Keep RPG skill entries and tactical ratings side by side. They are related, but not identical in play.
- Use specialties for flavor only after they are recorded and approved. They should narrow a weapon type, platform habit, or operating style.
- Clan, military, academy, rank, vehicle, and equipment traits can explain why a character has access or status, but the actual roll still needs the appropriate skill.
- For early play, record "Needs source review" rather than overfitting full BattleTech ratings before the player actually needs tactical combat.

## Common Edge Cases

- Some support skills are more important than Piloting or Gunnery outside combat. A convoy scene may hinge on Navigation, Technician, Communications, Negotiation, or Security Systems.
- A vehicle can have separate driver, gunner, commander, artillery, and sensor roles. Do not assume one PC rolls everything unless the vehicle and scene support that.
- Battle armor users may need both personal-combat skills and vehicle-scale skills depending on target and weapon.
- Special Pilot Abilities are closer to earned tactical traits than ordinary skills. They are not automatic rewards for high skill alone.
- Vehicle, Equipped, Property, Rank, Wealth, and Reputation traits can affect access, authority, ownership, and consequences, but exact trait tables remain source lookup.
