# Piloting

## Purpose

Use this summary when a character controls a BattleMech, IndustrialMech, battle armor suit, aerospace unit, ProtoMech, spacecraft, or other platform covered by Piloting rather than ordinary Driving.

## When to Use

- A pilot tries a risky maneuver, landing, jump, evasive move, recovery from bad terrain, or control check.
- A BattleMech, battle armor suit, aerospace unit, ProtoMech, or spacecraft needs RPG-scale operator competence.
- Tactical combat needs a character's A Time of War Piloting subskill recorded for handoff or conversion.
- A Special Pilot Ability, heat pressure, physical attack, fall, landing, or vehicle damage could affect pilot control.

## Do Not Use For

- Routine operation with no meaningful chance of failure.
- Ground, rail, or sea vehicles that the skill catalog routes to Driving instead.
- Firing mounted weapons. Use `rules/vehicles-and-mechs/gunnery.md`, Artillery, Support Weapons, Small Arms, or another specific skill as appropriate.
- Full tactical resolution of movement points, facing, terrain costs, heat, piloting skill rolls, falls, or aerospace movement. Use the tactical handoff and external tactical rules.

## Basic Procedure

1. Confirm that the platform uses Piloting. The important subskills are Aerospace, Air Vehicle, Battlesuit, 'Mech, ProtoMech, and Spacecraft; Ground Vehicle, Rail Vehicle, and Sea Vehicle route to Driving.
2. Confirm the exact subskill on the character sheet. Treat each subskill as its own practical discipline.
3. State the risk before rolling: skid, fall, bad landing, collision, lost time, exposed position, missed extraction, heat pressure, passenger danger, or tactical handoff.
4. Use the normal core skill-check process with the Piloting skill's linked attributes and target number from the private source.
5. Apply only source-supported situational modifiers. If using Total Warfare or Tactical Operations modifiers inside A Time of War, remember that the tactical addendum reverses them into roll modifiers.
6. On success, grant the maneuver and apply margin to quality, speed, position, or avoided damage only where the scene needs it.
7. On failure, choose a consequence that fits the established risk, then decide whether the result now requires tactical handoff.

## Practical GM Guidance

- Piloting checks are for pressure. Marching a BattleMech across an empty tarmac is not a check; racing, evading fire, bad footing, landing under stress, or damaged controls are checks.
- Treat vehicle category as more important than flavor. A MechWarrior with Piloting/'Mech does not automatically pilot a VTOL, DropShip, or battlesuit well.
- If the scene is mostly about getting from one place to another, one check is usually enough.
- If multiple units, movement order, terrain cost, facing, heat, or weapon timing matter, stop and create a tactical encounter instead of approximating from memory.
- For battle armor, choose scale carefully. Piloting/Battlesuit may matter for suit operation, but attacks against ordinary infantry often route back to personal combat.

## Common Edge Cases

- Special Pilot Abilities are earned later through XP and GM approval, generally not taken during normal character creation. They have prerequisites and class limits; do not grant them from concept alone.
- Heat in the tactical addendum is detailed and interacts with movement and attacks. If heat matters, hand off to tactical play.
- Tactical jumping has specific timing, distance, height, landing, and attack interactions. Use this summary only to decide that the pilot's skill matters; use the source or tactical tool for exact handling.
- Pilot or crew injury from overheating, falls, or critical effects has its own tactical bridge. Record injury consequences through personal combat and campaign recovery summaries after the tactical result is known.
- Driving covers common ground, rail, and sea vehicles even if they are military assets.
