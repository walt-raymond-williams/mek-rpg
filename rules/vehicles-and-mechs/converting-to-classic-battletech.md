# Converting to Classic BattleTech

## Purpose

Use this summary when an A Time of War character needs a Classic BattleTech, Total Warfare, MegaMek, or MekHQ pilot/gunnery note for tactical play.

## When to Use

- A vehicle, BattleMech, aerospace, battle armor, or tactical encounter is being handed off from RPG mode.
- A character has A Time of War Piloting, Driving, or Gunnery skill levels and the tactical tool needs Pilot/Gunnery-style ratings.
- A tactical unit has known Total Warfare ratings and the GM needs temporary A Time of War skill levels for RPG-scale checks.
- The GM needs to record crew roles, tactical skill users, or unresolved conversion assumptions before play.

## Do Not Use For

- Replacing full Total Warfare, Classic BattleTech, MegaMek, or MekHQ unit setup.
- Exact tactical rules for movement, heat, weapon attacks, armor, critical hits, initiative, or damage.
- Personal combat or non-vehicle skill checks.
- Inventing missing attributes, traits, wounds, SPAs, or equipment effects where tactical setup needs the source or a tool.

## Basic Procedure

1. Identify the relevant A Time of War skill and subskill:
   - `Gunnery/'Mech` for BattleMech mounted weapons.
   - `Piloting/'Mech` for BattleMech operation.
   - the matching Gunnery, Piloting, or Driving subskill for other platforms.
2. For an A Time of War character going into Total Warfare-style tactical play, subtract the character's relevant skill level from that skill's A Time of War target number to get the tactical rating.
3. For a tactical unit rating coming back into A Time of War, subtract the tactical rating from the matching A Time of War skill target number to get the RPG skill level.
4. If attributes or traits are unknown for a tactical-only pilot, use the source's generic assumption only as a temporary bridge and mark the sheet as incomplete.
5. Identify who makes which roll in a crewed unit: commander, driver/pilot, gunner, artillery operator, sensor operator, or squad leader.
6. Record conversion notes in the encounter or asset file: source skill, target number used, resulting tactical rating, assumptions, injuries, SPAs, and tool-specific setup notes.

## Practical GM Guidance

- This conversion is a bridge, not a character builder. Keep the tactical rating and the RPG skill entry visible so later edits can be corrected.
- Better A Time of War skill levels produce lower tactical ratings because Classic BattleTech-style target numbers are lower-is-better.
- Do not guess modifiers from the tactical game during live RPG play. The tactical addendum says Total Warfare modifiers invert when used as A Time of War roll modifiers.
- If the tactical encounter is important, set it up in MegaMek, MekHQ, or tabletop rules and bring the aftermath back into campaign state.
- For simple narrative scenes, a single RPG skill check is usually faster than conversion.

## Common Edge Cases

- Different Inner Sphere and Clan skill target numbers may matter when converting. Verify the correct source entry if exact numbers matter.
- Crewed vehicles may split actions across multiple characters. A commander may handle initiative or command while a driver moves and a gunner fires.
- Infantry cohesion and battle armor squads have averaging and leader-handling rules in the tactical addendum; do not improvise them from memory.
- Total Warfare-scale damage to infantry with named PCs can require a damage bridge back to A Time of War. Use the cited pages and record the result in personal-combat and campaign recovery files.
- Special Pilot Abilities and traits can affect tactical setup, but exact effects require source lookup.
