# Wounds

## Purpose

Wound rules describe the ongoing effects of damage: injury penalties, fatigue penalties, consciousness checks, bleeding, death, tactical kills, stun, and trait interactions.

## When to Use

- A character has marked standard or fatigue damage.
- A character takes standard damage or is hit by a subduing attack and may lose consciousness.
- A damaging hit is severe enough to risk bleeding.
- The GM needs to know whether injury or fatigue modifies later actions.
- The table needs a quick ruling on stun, death, or removal from a large fight.

## Do Not Use For

- Calculating attack damage before it is marked.
- Long-term healing, medical treatment, and surgery after the scene.
- Optional hit-location wound effects unless those optional rules are active.
- Kid-friendly narration of severe injury without applying the table's safety expectations.

## Basic Procedure

1. After damage is marked, calculate the character's injury modifier from standard damage relative to total standard damage capacity.
2. Apply injury modifiers to physical actions, consciousness checks, knockdown checks, movement rates, and medical checks made on the character, as appropriate.
3. Calculate fatigue modifiers from fatigue that exceeds the character's Willpower.
4. Apply fatigue modifiers to the character's Action Checks and consciousness checks, but not to movement rates or medical checks made by someone else.
5. When standard damage or a successful subduing attack requires it, make a consciousness check using the source procedure.
6. If a single standard-damage injury exceeds the bleeding threshold, make the source bleeding check.
7. If bleeding begins, track it as continuous damage in the End Phase until stopped with medical attention.
8. Mark stun when damage triggers it; clearing stun requires the source action cost.
9. If standard damage exceeds the character's standard damage capacity, the character dies unless an optional or campaign rule changes that outcome.

## Practical GM Guidance

Separate the immediate hit from the ongoing state. After resolving damage, tell the player: current standard damage, current fatigue, whether they are stunned, whether they remain conscious, and any obvious bleeding or mobility problem.

For player characters, pause before applying fatal consequences in kid-friendly mode or when the campaign tone calls for capture, tactical kill, or medical emergency instead. The rule summary records the default procedure; the table still controls tone.

## Common Edge Cases

- Injury penalties worsen in bands as standard damage fills the character's capacity.
- Fatigue penalties begin only after fatigue exceeds Willpower.
- Consciousness checks combine Willpower, Body, and current injury/fatigue state under the source procedure.
- A character can be only stunned or not stunned; repeated hits do not stack additional stun states.
- A character can be only bleeding or not bleeding under the simplified rules.
- In large combats, an unconscious combatant can be treated as a tactical kill and removed from the active fight, with survival checked later if bleeding or serious harm is involved.
- Traits such as Fit, Glass Jaw, Handicap, Toughness, and Pain Resistance can change damage, fatigue, stun, or injury effects.
