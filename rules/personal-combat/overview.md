# Personal Combat Overview

## Purpose

Personal combat is the RPG-scale combat procedure for character fights, creature attacks, close firefights, brawls, and other scenes where individual people matter more than BattleMech tactical detail.

## When to Use

- A fight is happening at personal scale.
- The scene needs structured combat turns instead of one core Action Check.
- Character position, cover, movement, attacks, wounds, fatigue, or recovery matter.
- The fight is small enough to track on a map, with miniatures, or by careful freeform notes.

## Do Not Use For

- Full BattleTech tactical combat with hex-facing, heat, armor locations, weapon range brackets, vehicles, or multiple units per side.
- One-roll dramatic violence where detailed turn order, distance, and damage tracking would slow the scene.
- Vehicle-scale or BattleMech-scale damage resolution.
- Optional hit locations, morale, knockdown, or lethality-reduction rules unless the GM explicitly turns those options on and checks the cited source pages.

## Basic Procedure

1. Confirm the scene belongs in personal combat rather than tactical BattleTech.
2. Establish participants, sides, starting positions, cover, visible weapons, and scale.
3. If using a map, agree on distance scale before the first turn.
4. Run each five-second combat turn in order: Initiative Phase, Action Phase, then End Phase.
5. During the Initiative Phase, determine acting order for individuals, squads, or teams.
6. During the Action Phase, resolve movement, attacks, skill use, and other declared actions in initiative order.
7. During the End Phase, resolve continuous damage, fatigue, extended actions, and automatic events.
8. Repeat turns until the fight ends, the opposition withdraws or surrenders, the scene changes, or a tactical handoff becomes necessary.

## Practical GM Guidance

Use personal combat when the important questions are who acts first, who reaches cover, who gets hit, and how badly a character is hurt. For small fights, freeform tracking can work if the GM is disciplined about distances, line of sight, and movement. For anything busy, use a simple map.

Before the first roll, tell the players what is immediately visible: range bands, cover, exits, who has weapons ready, and what tactical objective matters. Personal combat should still be about objectives, pressure, and choices, not only reducing damage tracks.

## Common Edge Cases

- Personal combat can include vehicles or battle armor only if they are being handled at the RPG scene scale. If their tactical movement, armor, or weapon rules matter, switch systems.
- The combat chapter assumes more structure than ordinary Basic Gameplay. Noncombat skill use still uses normal Action Check rules, but the GM may add stress, interruption, or time pressure.
- Maps are recommended because movement and line of sight are important. Freeform play is best reserved for small fights with clear positions.
- Character combat data includes condition, movement, armor, and weapon notes; equipment details still belong in equipment summaries and the legally owned source.
