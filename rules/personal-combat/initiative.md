# Initiative

## Purpose

Initiative determines who acts first during a personal-combat turn.

## When to Use

- A personal-combat turn begins.
- Two or more combatants might interfere with each other before everyone acts.
- The GM needs to choose between individual, squad, or team action order.
- A character wants to delay and react later in the same turn.

## Do Not Use For

- Basic noncombat checks where acting order is not contested.
- Classic BattleTech, MegaMek, MekHQ, or Tactical Combat Addendum initiative.
- Simultaneous-action tactical exchanges. Standard personal combat resolves actions immediately when a character acts.

## Basic Procedure

1. Choose the initiative scale:
   - individual initiative for small fights,
   - squad initiative for organized groups,
   - team initiative for large fights or vehicle-heavy scenes.
2. Roll the initiative dice for each acting character, squad leader, or team leader.
3. Apply only initiative-specific traits and allowed squad/team skill modifiers. Do not apply ordinary injury or fatigue penalties.
4. Sort from highest result to lowest result.
5. Break individual ties with Reflexes. For squad or team ties, use the relevant leader's Willpower plus Leadership.
6. If the tie remains unresolved, roll again between the tied actors.
7. Resolve actions immediately in that order during the Action Phase.
8. A higher-initiative actor may hold action, then act later in the same turn. Held actions expire at the end of the turn.

## Practical GM Guidance

Use individual initiative when the fight is intimate and every actor matters. Use squad or team initiative when the goal is to keep a larger fight moving without tracking every trooper separately.

Held actions are useful for overwatch, waiting for a door to open, or letting an ally move first. When a held actor interrupts, resolve all of that actor's actions at that moment, then return to the previous order.

## Common Edge Cases

- A held action can interrupt another character's action after the holder's original initiative point has passed.
- A held action does not carry into the next turn.
- Squad and team initiative depend on an active leader who can communicate with the unit.
- Team initiative should alternate sub-units proportionally rather than letting a much larger force move every unit before the smaller side can respond.
- Injury and fatigue do not modify initiative, even though they affect many other checks.
