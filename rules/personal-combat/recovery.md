# Recovery

## Purpose

Recovery rules handle stabilizing, healing, fatigue recovery, assisted medical care, and surgery after personal-scale injury.

## When to Use

- Combat has ended and an injured character needs stabilization or treatment.
- A character is bleeding, badly wounded, or too injured for ordinary rest to be enough.
- The table needs to decide whether normal healing, assisted healing, or surgery applies.
- A character is recovering fatigue after or during a fight.
- Injury recovery should create downtime, cost, risk, favors, or mission pressure.

## Do Not Use For

- Immediate attack damage resolution during the Action Phase.
- Tactical BattleTech pilot damage or unit repair.
- Exact surgery success table reproduction or medical equipment catalog lookup.
- Major specific wound surgery unless the optional hit-location/specific-wound rules are active and the cited source is checked.

## Basic Procedure

1. Stabilize the character before long-term healing. This usually means stopping blood loss, securing damaged body parts, and making the character safe to move.
2. Use the source medical check procedure for stabilization, applying the patient's injury state and situational modifiers.
3. Decide whether normal healing is allowed. Normal healing requires rest and basic safe conditions, and is not enough for major trauma or damage beyond the source threshold.
4. If normal healing applies, recover standard damage over weekly healing cycles, modified by conditions and relevant traits.
5. If assisted healing applies, require proper medical facilities and recurring medical care, then recover damage faster under the source procedure.
6. If surgery is required, resolve one specific injury per surgery attempt and apply the source success/failure levels.
7. Recover fatigue separately. Active rest and full rest recover fatigue at different rates; in-combat fatigue recovery uses the End Phase procedure.
8. Update campaign state with current damage, fatigue, lasting injuries, treatment status, downtime, and any medical costs or obligations.

## Practical GM Guidance

Use recovery as a campaign consequence, not just bookkeeping. An injured MechWarrior may need a clinic, a favor from a faction doctor, time in hiding, replacement gear, or a hard choice about launching the next mission wounded.

When the injury is severe, tell the table what is known and what needs source inspection: stabilization now, hospital later, surgery risk, downtime length, and whether any lasting trait or impairment may result.

## Common Edge Cases

- Normal healing is for injuries that can plausibly recover with rest and safe conditions; major trauma or very high damage requires assisted care or surgery.
- Strenuous activity during recovery can slow or break the healing process.
- Assisted healing requires both facilities and regular medical attention; losing either can revert recovery to normal healing.
- Surgery is limited by time, facility quality, patient condition, and the surgeon's skill.
- A failed surgery can still stabilize a wound in some cases, but may leave lasting effects; severe failure can create immediate danger.
- Corrective surgery may be possible before healing is complete, but it carries the risk of a worse result.
- Fatigue clears far faster than standard damage and should not be conflated with healing wounds.
