# End Phase

## Purpose

The End Phase closes a personal-combat turn by resolving ongoing damage, fatigue, extended actions, and automatic events that were not completed during the Action Phase.

## When to Use

- Every personal-combat turn reaches its final phase.
- A character is bleeding, burning, poisoned, suffocating, drowning, or under another continuous effect.
- Fatigue from exertion, movement, encumbrance, traits, or damage must be updated.
- A character spends a turn recovering fatigue.
- The GM needs to resolve an extended action or automatic scene event.

## Do Not Use For

- Immediate attack rolls or damage rolls during the Action Phase.
- Long-term healing after combat has ended.
- Morale checks, hit locations, knockdown, or other optional personal-combat rules unless the GM has turned them on and checks the cited pages.

## Basic Procedure

1. At the end of the turn, resolve bleeding and other continuous damage.
2. After continuous damage is applied, make any required consciousness checks.
3. Apply fatigue gained from movement, exertion, encumbrance, damage side effects, and trait multipliers.
4. If a conscious character spent the turn recovering fatigue, reduce fatigue by the source amount.
5. If a character is unconscious, apply the source unconscious fatigue recovery during the End Phase.
6. Check whether extended actions need progress, interruption, or a roll during the next Action Phase.
7. Resolve automatic events such as timers, moving platforms, environmental changes, or other neutral scene developments.
8. Begin the next turn with a new Initiative Phase if combat continues.

## Practical GM Guidance

Use a consistent end-of-turn checklist. The main risk is forgetting ongoing effects after the exciting attack has already happened.

For live play, write down every continuous effect with its source, damage type, and stop condition. If the source is table-heavy, cite the page and inspect it instead of guessing.

## Common Edge Cases

- Continuous damage can trigger consciousness checks after it is applied.
- Recovering fatigue in combat takes the character's full turn and sharply limits other action.
- Unconscious characters recover fatigue during the End Phase, but that does not automatically remove standard damage or other dangers.
- Extended actions may not roll in the End Phase; the GM may simply decide whether they continue or need a roll on the next action.
- Optional morale rules begin near this section but are outside this issue's minimum summary scope.
