# Ranged Attacks

## Purpose

Ranged attack rules resolve personal-scale attacks made with firearms, bows, thrown weapons, grenades, support weapons, and similar attacks against targets at least one meter away.

## When to Use

- A character attacks with a personal ranged weapon.
- A thrown weapon or explosive is used at personal scale.
- Range, cover, line of sight, movement, careful aim, burst fire, suppression, indirect fire, or blind fire matters.
- The attack should produce damage, stun, fatigue, or a miss during personal combat.

## Do Not Use For

- Melee attacks or point-blank fighting where combatants are engaged in close combat.
- BattleMech, vehicle, aerospace, or tactical-scale weapon fire.
- Exact weapon statistics, ammunition lists, or copied combat modifier tables; inspect the cited source and equipment pages when exact values matter.
- Optional hit-location aimed shots unless that optional rule is in play.

## Basic Procedure

1. Confirm the target is at least one meter away and within the weapon's usable range.
2. Confirm line of sight unless the attack is blind fire, indirect fire, or another source-defined exception.
3. Choose the appropriate ranged weapon skill or the appropriate untrained Attribute Check.
4. Start with the skill's target number and normal Skill Check modifiers.
5. Add personal-combat modifiers for range, cover, attacker movement, target movement, target size, attacker condition, special attack mode, and environmental conditions.
6. Roll the attack as a Skill-based Action Check.
7. On success, expend ammunition or energy and route to `rules/personal-combat/damage.md`.
8. On failure, still expend ammunition or energy and resolve any source-defined miss behavior for special attacks such as indirect area fire.

## Practical GM Guidance

Before the player rolls, name the tactical facts that matter: range band, target cover, movement, visibility, and whether allies are in melee with the target. If the player wants better odds, offer concrete options such as taking careful aim, moving to a new angle, suppressing an area, or waiting with a held action.

Do not recreate the combat modifier table in play notes. Use the summary to choose the right procedure, then inspect the cited page if the exact modifier matters.

## Common Edge Cases

- Point-blank range is still a ranged attack if the combatants are not engaged in melee; once they are engaged, use melee procedures.
- Full cover may still allow blind fire or indirect fire if the attack type allows it, but aimed shots and careful aim generally require seeing the target.
- Area-effect attacks apply damage by proximity to the impact point and can affect multiple targets.
- Burst fire can trade accuracy for extra potential damage and enables suppression fire.
- Crewed weapons require the needed crew nearby and setup before they can attack.
- Indirect fire can use a spotter, communication, and scatter on a miss.
- Subduing ranged attacks inflict fatigue rather than standard damage.
