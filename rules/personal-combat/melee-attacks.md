# Melee Attacks

## Purpose

Melee attack rules resolve personal-scale close combat with fists, feet, blades, clubs, grapples, and ranged weapons used while engaged in melee.

## When to Use

- Combatants are fighting at close personal range.
- A character attacks or defends with Martial Arts, Melee Weapons, or another appropriate close-combat skill.
- A grapple, blind melee attack, subduing strike, or ranged weapon used in melee matters.
- The defender's response should affect whether one side, both sides, or neither side takes damage.

## Do Not Use For

- Ranged attacks against targets outside melee.
- BattleMech physical attacks or tactical-scale infantry combat.
- Optional hit locations, knockdown, or detailed aimed strikes unless the GM has turned those rules on.
- Exact melee modifier table lookup from memory; use the cited source page when precision matters.

## Basic Procedure

1. Confirm the combatants are close enough for melee.
2. Identify the attacker's skill and the defender's defense skill. The skills may differ.
3. Resolve the attack as an Opposed Skill Check.
4. Apply personal-combat modifiers to both rolls. Each side treats itself as the acting combatant for its own movement and condition modifiers.
5. Compare success and margins:
   - attacker succeeds with enough margin: attacker deals melee damage,
   - attacker succeeds but does not beat the defender's margin: both sides take damage,
   - attacker fails and defender succeeds: defender counterstrikes and deals damage,
   - both fail: neither side deals damage.
6. Route any damage to `rules/personal-combat/damage.md`.
7. If a grapple was declared, apply the grapple result instead of normal damage when the source conditions are met.

## Practical GM Guidance

Melee should feel immediate. Ask what the attacker is trying to accomplish: injure, restrain, drive back, subdue, break away, or create space. Then pick the appropriate melee procedure.

Because melee uses opposed checks, both combatants are dangerous. Tell the player before the roll when a failed attack could let the defender hurt them back.

## Common Edge Cases

- No more than the source limit of attackers can make melee attacks against the same target in a turn.
- Ordinary melee defense rolls are incidental actions, so defending does not normally consume the defender's later attack.
- Grapples restrain rather than deal damage, and maintaining or breaking a grapple has its own opposed procedure.
- A ranged weapon used while engaged in melee is handled as an opposed close-combat action, not as ordinary ranged fire.
- Blind fighting prevents aimed shots and can involve barrier rules if the attack passes through an obstruction.
- Unarmed and other subduing melee attacks generally produce fatigue rather than standard damage.
