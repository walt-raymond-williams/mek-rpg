# Factions And History

## Purpose

Use this routing aid when the table needs brief campaign orientation for BattleTech factions, broad historical eras, and where a PC or mission sits in the setting.

## When to Use

- A player asks who the major powers are or what faction controls a situation.
- The GM needs to place a mission in an era, border region, invasion corridor, Periphery area, Clan context, or Jihad-era pressure.
- A faction, noble house, Clan, ComStar, Word of Blake, mercenary unit, or Periphery power matters to scene framing.
- The table needs to decide what is table canon, what needs source lookup, and what can be improvised.

## Do Not Use For

- Full faction essays, detailed historical timelines, or world-by-world canon.
- Treating a brief orientation as authoritative canon for a specific date or planet.
- Copying faction descriptions, historical prose, or sourcebook setting text.
- Resolving faction reputation mechanics. Use `rules/campaign/reputation.md`.

## Basic Procedure

1. Identify the campaign era and current table-canon assumptions from `campaign-state/setting-basics.md`.
2. Identify which setting layer matters:
   - major Inner Sphere state
   - Clan
   - Periphery or Deep Periphery power
   - ComStar or Word of Blake
   - local planetary government, military, corporation, or noble house
3. State only the facts needed for the scene: current allegiance, likely pressure, local authority, and why the PCs care.
4. Mark canon-heavy details as `Lookup needed` unless they are already present in the active campaign save.
5. Route terminology questions through `indexes/term-glossary.md`.
6. Record any table-canon decision in the active campaign save or `campaign-state/setting-basics.md`.

## Practical GM Guidance

- For live play, a faction usually needs a motive, a face, a resource, and a consequence. It does not need a full history lesson.
- Use broad faction identity to create pressure, but let local NPCs differ from stereotype when that makes the scene stronger.
- When a source answer would affect mission legality, military rank, noble title, succession, or faction reputation, pause and inspect the source or a campaign-approved canon reference.
- Keep the Jihad-era and Clan material as campaign context unless the active campaign has explicitly chosen a different era.
