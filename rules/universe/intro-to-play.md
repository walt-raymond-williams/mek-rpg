# Intro To Play

## Purpose

Use this routing aid when a player needs a table-facing explanation of what this BattleTech RPG workspace is for, what roles the players and GM have, what materials are expected, or how A Time of War relates to roleplaying in the BattleTech setting.

## When to Use

- A new player asks what kind of game this is.
- The group needs a reminder of GM and player responsibilities.
- A scene needs a quick distinction between player characters, NPCs, rules, dice, imagination, and record keeping.
- The GM is choosing which rule chapter or workspace file to consult before play.

## Do Not Use For

- Detailed BattleTech lore, faction history, timeline answers, or canon debates.
- Character creation, combat, equipment, or advancement procedures.
- Reproducing the introductory prose, examples, glossary text, or book chapter descriptions.

## Basic Procedure

1. Frame the game as character-centered roleplay in the BattleTech universe, not as a replacement for full tactical BattleTech.
2. Identify the active roles:
   - players control their PCs and choose actions.
   - the GM frames situations, portrays NPCs, judges uncertainty, and applies rules when needed.
3. Confirm the table materials needed for the session: character records, dice or dice roller, campaign notes, and the relevant rules routes.
4. Route rules questions through `indexes/task-router.md` instead of answering from memory.
5. Track persistent changes in the active campaign save folder when play creates durable consequences.
6. Switch to Classic BattleTech, MegaMek, or MekHQ when exact tactical combat becomes the main activity.

## Practical GM Guidance

- Keep the first explanation practical: who the PCs are, what problem is in front of them, and what choices matter now.
- Treat rules as support for the table's decisions. Roll when the outcome is uncertain and failure matters.
- Use the workspace's campaign files as the continuity layer, especially after missions, NPC conversations, injuries, rewards, and faction consequences.
- If a new player needs setting context, give a few campaign-relevant facts and route deeper canon questions to `rules/universe/factions-and-history.md` and source lookup.
