# Character Record Basics

## Purpose

Use this summary to review what a character record is supposed to hold before play: personal identity, attributes, traits, skills, combat data, inventory, biography, and vehicle notes. This is a sheet-completeness aid, not a replacement for the character sheet or source tables.

## When to Use

- A PC or important NPC sheet needs a pre-session completeness pass.
- The GM needs to know which sheet fields matter during play.
- A campaign save needs to record character facts without copying a source record sheet.
- A player is unsure where traits, skills, damage, movement, inventory, or vehicle links belong.

## Do Not Use For

- Copying or recreating the source record sheet layout.
- Resolving checks. Use `rules/core/action-checks.md`, `rules/core/attribute-checks.md`, or `rules/core/skill-checks.md`.
- Building a character from Life Modules, points-only purchases, traits, or skills. Use `rules/character-creation/overview.md`.
- Reproducing sample characters, tables, damage tracks, armor tables, weapon stats, or vehicle records.

## Basic Procedure

1. Record personal data first: character name, controlling player if any, affiliation, physical description, and any table-facing identity notes.
2. Record all eight attributes and their current scores. Keep earned or assigned XP visible when the sheet is still under construction or advancement matters.
3. Record traits with enough detail to adjudicate them later: trait name, trait points, sign, descriptor or identity when needed, page reference, and current XP if incomplete.
4. Record skills with exact subskill and specialty notation, level, linked attributes, target number/complexity, and any XP stored toward the next level.
5. Record combat data separately from general character data: condition or damage tracking, fatigue, stun/unconscious status, movement rates, worn armor, ready weapons, and combat-relevant notes.
6. Record biographical data as campaign hooks: origin, affiliation, education, career stages, debts, relationships, enemies, loyalties, and motives.
7. Record inventory as play-facing equipment, including where important gear is kept. Use equipment summaries and source pages for exact item stats.
8. Record vehicle data only when the character has a meaningful vehicle trait, assigned unit, owned asset, or tactical handoff link. Keep exact tactical unit handling outside this summary.

## Practical GM Guidance

- Treat the record as a play tool. If a field will not affect choices, rolls, consequences, or campaign memory, do not overwork it during live play.
- Mark incomplete areas as `Needs source review`, `Needs GM ruling`, or `Needs user decision` instead of inventing values.
- For MEK-RPG campaign saves, a compact Markdown character section is acceptable as long as it preserves the important sheet categories and unresolved questions.
- Keep sheet examples original. Do not copy source sample characters or the source record-sheet layout into committed files.
- Vehicle notes should identify ownership or assignment and route tactical details to `gm/switch-to-classic-battletech.md`, MegaMek, or MekHQ when unit combat matters.

## Common Edge Cases

- A character can have XP stored toward an attribute, trait, or skill without the next level being active. Record stored XP separately from active value.
- Subskills are separate practical competencies. Do not merge `Piloting/Mech`, `Piloting/Spacecraft`, and `Driving/Ground Vehicle` into one generic entry.
- Specialties are narrower than subskills and should be written in parentheses after the skill or subskill.
- Identity-based traits need descriptors showing which alias, property, reputation, enemy, connection, obligation, or resource they belong to.
- Vehicle traits and vehicle data do not make MEK-RPG the tactical source of truth. Use the campaign asset sheet and tactical handoff docs for larger unit state.
