# Opposed Actions

## Purpose

Opposed Actions resolve direct contests where another character's action resists, blocks, races, or counters the acting character. The winner is determined by comparing successful margins rather than by checking each side in isolation.

## When to Use

- Two characters directly compete for the same outcome.
- One character's action is actively resisted by another character's Skill or Attribute.
- The opposition can be symmetric, such as a strength contest, or asymmetric, such as Stealth against Perception or willpower against intimidation.

## Do Not Use For

- Passive difficulty with no active opposing character; use a normal Action Check.
- Tactical combat exchanges that have their own attack, defense, movement, and damage procedures.
- Group contests where the table needs a larger subsystem for teams, vehicles, or tactical units.

## Basic Procedure

1. Define what each side wants and what happens if neither side gains a clear result.
2. Choose the appropriate Attribute Check or Skill Check for each side.
3. Apply target numbers and modifiers as for ordinary Action Checks.
4. Roll for both sides.
5. If one side succeeds and the other fails, the successful side wins; use the failed side's margin of failure to help judge net margin.
6. If both sides succeed, the higher Margin of Success wins.
7. If both sides tie on margin, or both fail without a clear consequential difference, spend the time and energy in the fiction but do not grant a clean win.

## Practical GM Guidance

Make the stakes mutual before rolling. A good opposed check answers a concrete question such as "Do you slip past the sentry?" or "Who controls the negotiation?" If both sides fail, use the fiction: hesitation, confusion, a noisy scramble, lost time, or mutual embarrassment may be more useful than simply rerolling.

## Common Edge Cases

- Different Skills and Attributes can oppose each other when the fiction supports it.
- Tied successful margins do not automatically favor the initiating character.
- If both sides fail badly, the result can include collateral consequences, but keep those consequences proportional to the scene.
- Edge can affect a roll only within the limits in `rules/core/edge.md`.
