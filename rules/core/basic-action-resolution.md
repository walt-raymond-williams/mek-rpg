# Basic Action Resolution

## Purpose

Basic Action Resolution explains how to turn an Attribute Check, Skill Check, or Opposed Action into a result: roll dice, apply modifiers, compare to a target number, handle special natural rolls, and interpret margin.

## When to Use

- Any core Action Check needs the actual roll resolved.
- The GM needs to apply Attribute, Skill, linked Attribute, specialization, difficulty, condition, injury, fatigue, or environmental modifiers.
- The degree of success or failure matters.
- A natural very low or very high roll may change the outcome.

## Do Not Use For

- Creating the character traits, Skill values, or equipment statistics that feed into the roll.
- Reproducing the source tables in notes or chat; cite the relevant source page instead.
- Full tactical BattleTech resolution, where target-number and modifier conventions differ from the RPG procedure.

## Basic Procedure

1. Determine the target number from the check type or the Skill entry.
2. Roll 2D6 unless a specific trait or condition changes the dice procedure.
3. Add the appropriate modifiers.
4. Treat positive modifiers as bonuses to the roll result and negative modifiers as penalties to the roll result.
5. Success occurs when the final modified result equals or exceeds the target number.
6. If the natural dice roll is the lowest possible 2D6 result, handle it as an automatic failure even if modifiers would otherwise succeed.
7. If the natural dice roll is the highest possible 2D6 result, check whether the high-roll procedure grants additional dice before finalizing the result.
8. Calculate Margin of Success or Margin of Failure as the distance between the final result and the target number.
9. For opposed actions, calculate net margin by comparing the winner's margin against the opponent's success or failure margin.

## Practical GM Guidance

Use modifiers to express the most important fictional factors, not every possible detail. The source includes tables for common check types, difficulty, vision, injury, fatigue, planetary conditions, and broad good or bad working conditions; when exact values matter, cite the table page and inspect the legally owned source.

Margin is a pacing tool. A small margin can mean success with flaws, delay, limited information, or minor cost. A large margin can mean faster work, better quality, more information, or stronger leverage. A large failure can create sharper complications, but avoid turning every failed noncombat check into damage unless the stakes already made that risk clear.

## Common Edge Cases

- A natural 2 is a failure even if bonuses would otherwise meet the target.
- A natural 12 can open a high-roll procedure; it does not guarantee success if the final result still cannot meet the task's demands unless the roll reaches the source-defined miraculous limit.
- Attribute Checks and trained Skill Checks use different modifier logic.
- A Time of War adds modifiers to the roll result; Classic BattleTech target-number conventions are different and should be handled through the tactical handoff.
- Do not copy the modifier or margin tables into committed files. Use page references.
