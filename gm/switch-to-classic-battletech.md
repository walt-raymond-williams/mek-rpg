# Switch to Classic BattleTech

Stop narrating RPG combat and generate a Classic BattleTech, MegaMek, or MekHQ encounter setup when tactical detail matters more than scene-level resolution.

Switch when one or more are true:

- BattleMechs, combat vehicles, aerospace units, or full squads are central to the fight.
- Facing, hex movement, terrain, heat, armor locations, ammunition, weapon range brackets, firing arcs, or critical hits matter.
- The players care about exact positioning or tactical survival.
- Multiple units on each side need initiative and turn-by-turn handling.
- The outcome should affect salvage, repairs, force strength, or campaign logistics.
- Vehicle-versus-vehicle or BattleMech-versus-vehicle attacks are more important than a single RPG-scale consequence.
- A tactical tool is needed to preserve unit state, damage, heat, ammo, repairs, or campaign force history.

Encounter setup should include:

- Map environment and terrain assumptions.
- Forces on each side.
- Pilot quality, crew roles, and RPG-derived pilot/gunnery notes from `rules/vehicles-and-mechs/`.
- Objectives and withdrawal conditions.
- Special rules or scenario constraints.
- What campaign state should be updated after the fight.
- Known damage, heat, ammunition, fuel, salvage, injury, and repair consequences to bring back into the campaign save.

Stay in RPG mode when the scene is a brief chase, intimidation, sabotage, escape, routine travel, vehicle purchase, crew assignment, or single decisive roll where tactical positioning is not important. Start vehicle lookups at `rules/vehicles-and-mechs/overview.md` before deciding whether this handoff applies.
