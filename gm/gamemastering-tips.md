# Gamemastering Tips

## Purpose

Use this GM procedure when preparing or adjusting a BattleTech RPG campaign, shaping a session around player choices, or keeping the setting useful without letting canon overwhelm play.

## When to Use

- Starting a campaign or checking whether the premise fits the players.
- Preparing a session with maps, NPCs, assets, likely opposition, or important consequences.
- Responding when players pursue a plan the GM did not expect.
- Turning BattleTech source material into usable scene pressure.

## Do Not Use For

- Reproducing GM advice prose, examples, or boxed text from the source.
- Replacing the scene loop or roll policy during live play.
- Forcing players down a prepared path.
- Settling exact rules or canon disputes without source lookup.

## Basic Procedure

1. Choose the campaign focus and confirm it interests the players.
2. Prepare the likely environment, NPCs, maps, assets, and opposition at the level of detail the session needs.
3. Track what the players did, who noticed, and what changed.
4. Let player plans matter. If they bypass a prepared obstacle, convert unused prep into consequences, later NPC reactions, or new hooks.
5. Use NPCs to reveal pressure, motives, and criticism in-world instead of forcing choices out of character.
6. Keep recurring details consistent: names, promises, wounds, debts, factions, locations, and prior rulings.
7. After the session, update the active campaign save and unresolved hooks.

## Practical GM Guidance

- Preparation is most valuable when it creates usable options: people to talk to, places to move through, threats to weigh, and consequences to accept.
- BattleTech canon is a tool. Decide what the campaign needs, then keep exact canon questions explicit as lookup-needed unless they affect a major outcome.
- When players seem uninterested, change the incentive or visible stakes rather than demanding they follow the original route.
- Give each player a chance to affect the scene. If one PC's specialty dominates, add a parallel social, technical, tactical, or moral pressure.
- Use feedback after play for recurring table problems; handle immediate safety or comfort issues immediately.
