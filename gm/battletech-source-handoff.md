# BattleTech Source Handoff

## Purpose

Use this GM procedure when a question belongs in another BattleTech product line, a sourcebook, a record sheet, a tactical rules set, fiction, or campaign canon rather than in this workspace's paraphrased A Time of War summaries.

## When to Use

- A player asks where to find exact BattleTech tactical rules, unit records, technical readouts, faction detail, or novel-style context.
- A mission needs Total Warfare, Tactical Operations, Strategic Operations, MegaMek, or MekHQ support.
- A table question depends on sourcebook canon, a technical readout, a record sheet, or a published era event.
- The GM needs to separate inspiration from rules authority.

## Do Not Use For

- Answering exact rules without reading the committed summaries or owned source.
- Reproducing technical readouts, record sheets, sourcebook lore, fiction, or product-line text.
- Treating fiction as a rules override.

## Basic Procedure

1. Classify the request:
   - RPG-scale rule or procedure
   - tactical BattleTech rule
   - unit or equipment record
   - faction, history, or setting canon
   - fiction or inspiration
2. For RPG-scale rules, start at `indexes/task-router.md`.
3. For tactical combat, use `gm/switch-to-classic-battletech.md` and `gm/tactical-encounter-handoff-checklist.md`.
4. For unit records or technical data, route to the appropriate private source, MegaMek, MekHQ, or official record source.
5. For faction or historical canon, decide whether the active campaign needs exact canon or a table-canon ruling.
6. For fiction, use it as inspiration unless the table has adopted it as campaign canon.

## Practical GM Guidance

- Keep authority explicit. Say whether an answer comes from a committed summary, private source lookup, campaign canon, an external tactical tool, or GM judgment.
- If a source handoff would stop play for a minor detail, make a temporary table ruling and record it as lookup-needed.
- If the handoff affects survival, major assets, tactical outcome, rank/title, faction standing, or campaign canon, inspect the source before locking the result.
