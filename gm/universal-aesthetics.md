# Universal Aesthetics

## Purpose

Use this GM procedure when a world, settlement, culture, political situation, technology level, or BattleMech presence needs to feel distinct at the table without requiring a full canon write-up.

## When to Use

- The PCs arrive on a new planet, city, base, battlefield, port, arena, noble estate, or frontier settlement.
- The GM needs quick sensory and social details for a scene.
- A mission depends on local politics, technology, environmental hostility, or restrictions on BattleMech use.
- The table needs a non-canon local culture that still feels compatible with BattleTech.

## Do Not Use For

- Reproducing world descriptions, faction essays, or setting prose.
- Inventing canon for named worlds when exact canon matters.
- Resolving environmental hazard rules. Use `rules/special/planetary-conditions.md`.
- Running full BattleMech tactical action.

## Basic Procedure

1. Pick the four visible layers:
   - world or environment
   - people and local culture
   - politics and authority
   - technology and infrastructure
2. Add one player-facing detail for each layer before the first decision point.
3. Decide whether BattleMechs, vehicles, weapons, armor, or off-world gear are normal, restricted, alarming, illegal, or impractical here.
4. Give the location one pressure that affects the mission: law, weather, poverty, wealth, surveillance, local custom, damaged infrastructure, or a hostile faction.
5. If a named world or canon feature matters, mark it for source lookup instead of improvising permanent canon.
6. Record durable local facts in the active campaign save.

## Practical GM Guidance

- A planet is large enough to support variety. Do not treat one scene as the whole world's only culture unless the campaign has established that.
- Local politics can create pressure without combat: permits, patrols, bribes, curfews, noble privilege, bureaucratic delays, or faction rivalries.
- Technology levels can vary within the same world. A starport, noble district, slum, factory, and wilderness settlement may all feel different.
- BattleMechs solve some problems and create others. Use physical access, law, collateral risk, maintenance, terrain, and social reaction to make dismounting meaningful.
