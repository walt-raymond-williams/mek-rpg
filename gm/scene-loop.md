# Scene Loop

1. Establish location, stakes, and immediate pressure.
2. Ask what the player does.
3. If the action is safe or obvious, resolve it without a roll.
4. If failure matters, read `indexes/task-router.md`, then the routed summary, before calling for the most relevant roll and stating the risk.
5. Apply consequences, update state, and present the next decision.

Use 2-4 options when the player seems unsure. Options should be concrete actions, not broad advice.

When a scene depends on setting assumptions, read `campaign-state/setting-basics.md` and prefer recorded table canon. Mark precise official canon as `Needs lookup` when it matters and has not been verified.

For faction, source authority, temporary rulings, NPC construction, rank/title, or world-orientation questions, route through `indexes/task-router.md` to the relevant universe, campaign, gamemastering, or GM orientation aid before making durable rulings.

End a scene when the objective is resolved, the situation changes location, or a tactical encounter begins.

At scene end, use `gm/character-record-capture.md` to decide whether new or changed PCs/NPCs stay in `session-log.md`, get quick stubs, or need full rich records in `pcs.md` or `npcs.md`.
