# Whistle Stop Tour

## Purpose

Use this routing aid when the GM wants example world-profile references from the source or needs a campaign-local world profile without copying published world descriptions.

## When to Use

- The GM wants to inspect source example planets for inspiration.
- The PCs visit a named or original world and the campaign needs a concise local profile.
- A location needs population, government, technology, industry, transport, culture, hazards, factions, or mission hooks.
- The table needs to decide whether a named-world detail is canon, table canon, or improvisable color.

## Do Not Use For

- Reproducing source world profiles, geography, history, government descriptions, economy text, or local color.
- Treating an example world as a template that must fit every planet.
- Replacing exact canon lookup for named worlds when the detail affects campaign outcomes.

## Routing Procedure

1. Decide whether the location is published canon, table canon, or original.
2. For published worlds, inspect the cited source pages or another approved canon reference before committing major facts.
3. For original or table-canon worlds, create a campaign-local profile with:
   - name and controlling authority
   - local government and law
   - settlement or terrain focus
   - technology and infrastructure
   - key industries, imports, and shortages
   - social pressure or cultural feature
   - military or security presence
   - active hooks and consequences
4. Store the resulting profile in active `campaigns/<campaign-id>/locations.md` or mission notes.
5. Keep source world profiles as inspiration and lookup, not committed summary text.

## Practical GM Guidance

- A useful world profile gives the PCs choices: where to go, who blocks them, what resources are scarce, and what consequences follow.
- Tie local facts to mission logistics. Weather, distance to port, customs, power supply, money, medicine, spare parts, and communications often matter more than encyclopedic history.
- Use one striking local detail per scene, then let player questions reveal more.
- If the campaign changes a published world, record that as table canon so future lookups do not silently overwrite play.
