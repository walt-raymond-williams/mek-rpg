# Transport And Large-Asset Source Coverage

Status: source-coverage map for issue `#52`. This is not a rules summary.

## Purpose

This note maps whether A Time of War appears to support DropShip, transport, and large-asset acquisition/ownership play. It uses the committed chapter map, existing paraphrased summaries, and targeted checks of the ignored extracted source text. It does not reproduce source tables, price lists, unit records, or raw source text.

## Playtest Gap

The Galatea DropShip playtest needed support for:

- DropShip purchase and negotiation
- ownership/title review and clean transfer
- liens, debts, co-op stakes, permits, and port bureaucracy
- inspection, maintenance defects, remote locks, and cleanup work
- crew loyalty, operating cost, fuel, and readiness
- what belongs to RPG scene handling versus MekHQ, MegaMek, Classic BattleTech, or table canon

## Gaps And Unsupported Areas

- No dedicated A Time of War DropShip purchase/title-transfer subsystem was found in the reviewed ranges.
- No source-backed lien, port permit, registration, insurance, docking fee, customs, cooperative-stake, or title-clearing procedure was found.
- No committed source support yet for exact DropShip prices, operating costs, crew payroll, berth fees, cargo income, loan amortization, or aerospace maintenance budget.
- Remote command locks, navigation lockouts, and inherited maintenance defects can be handled as RPG technical/security scenes, but their exact mechanics are table rulings unless another source is added.
- Force-level maintenance, contract economics, unit finance, campaign date, unit rosters, repairs, and market state should route to MekHQ when the campaign is MekHQ-linked.
- Full tactical aerospace/DropShip combat should route to Classic BattleTech, MegaMek, or MekHQ.
