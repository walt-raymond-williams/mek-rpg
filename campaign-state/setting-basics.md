## Table Canon

Treat these as established unless the user changes them:

- Game style: character-scale BattleTech RPG play using A Time of War style procedures.
- Tactical handoff: use Classic BattleTech, MegaMek, or MekHQ when hex-scale BattleMech combat matters.
- Rules source: committed paraphrased summaries and routing indexes first; source lookup only when summaries are insufficient.
- Campaign posture: playable, faction-aware, and kid-friendly when requested.
- Player agency: missions should offer meaningful choices, negotiation, investigation, salvage, rescue, retreat, and alliance consequences.

## Canon Policy

Use this policy during play:

- Table canon: anything recorded in this file, the selected `campaigns/<campaign-id>/` save folder, campaign-local session logs, or confirmed directly by the user. Preserve it even if later improvised color would be flashier.
- Lookup-needed canon: precise dates, named canon personalities, official unit histories, world ownership, major battles, technology availability by era, and faction borders. Ask for or perform lookup only when precision matters to the scene.
- Improvisable color: local NPCs, minor settlements, small contracts, personal grudges, local company names, cargo manifests, weather, rumors, and non-decisive background flavor.
- Conflict rule: if improvised color later conflicts with verified canon, keep the table-facing consequence and adjust the explanation unless the user prefers a retcon.
- Copyright rule: do not copy official lore text into campaign notes. Record short original summaries, page or source pointers where useful, and uncertainty labels.

Canon strictness options for the user to choose:

- Loose canon: use the setting as inspiration; keep play continuity above official detail.
- Guided canon: use official dates, factions, and broad events when known; improvise local detail freely.
- Strict canon: verify major setting facts before committing them and mark uncertain facts as `Needs lookup`.

Default until decided: Guided canon for broad faction flavor, with `Needs lookup` on precise facts.

## Starting Premise Options

Choose one as the first campaign frame, combine pieces, or replace them:

1. Border Contract
   - The player unit is hired to protect a lightly defended route where two larger powers deny responsibility for escalating raids.
   - Good for: patrols, investigation, tense negotiations, salvage, prisoner choices, and tactical handoffs when a raid becomes a BattleMech fight.

2. Canopian Medical Corridor
   - A humanitarian convoy, clinic, or rescue mission becomes politically valuable because several factions need it and none fully trust each other.
   - Good for: kid-friendly stakes, medical gear use, escort scenes, local mysteries, and faction bargaining.

3. Combine Defector Problem
   - A person, prototype component, or sealed data cache crosses a border and several groups want custody for different reasons.
   - Good for: honor pressure, social scenes, chase scenes, security, and difficult choices without requiring immediate lethal combat.

4. Periphery Salvage Claim
   - A recoverable battlefield site, lost depot, or damaged DropShip draws locals, mercenaries, raiders, and official claimants.
   - Good for: exploration, repair, negotiation, personal combat, and a clean handoff if heavy units enter the field.

5. Academy Field Assignment
   - Young or inexperienced characters are sent on a supervised training or courier job that becomes real when local trouble outgrows the lesson plan.
   - Good for: lower-lethality play, tutorial pacing, recurring mentors, and clear session objectives.

## First Session Hooks

- A routine checkpoint inspection turns up forged papers from two different factions.
- A damaged scout arrives with only part of a warning and a map coordinate.
- A local official offers payment for quiet help but refuses to put the job on record.
- A rival crew claims the same salvage rights and proposes a risky compromise.
- A family, clinic, or school needs evacuation before a larger force arrives.
- A patron asks the unit to verify a rumor before committing BattleMechs.

5. Ask the user for any open choice that is necessary for the next scene.
6. If a precise canon fact matters, mark it `Needs lookup` until verified.
