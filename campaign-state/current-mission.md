# Current Mission

Mission name: Checkpoint Ghosts

Status: Playtest scaffold for issue `#12`; not fixed table canon until the user accepts it.

## Premise

A routine checkpoint inspection finds two sets of forged transit papers pointing at different patrons. The player unit must decide whether to detain the courier, follow the handoff trail, warn the local clinic, or quietly sell the information.

This mission is designed to exercise the first playable GM loop with investigation, pressure, at least one opposed or skill roll, a possible character-scale confrontation, and a clear tactical handoff if heavy units appear.

## Open User Choices

- Era: Needs user decision.
- Starting region: Needs user decision; use an unnamed border world for the playtest if the user wants to start immediately.
- Player unit concept: Needs user decision; default playtest assumption is a small hired security or scout team.
- Canon strictness: Needs user decision; default to guided canon from `campaign-state/setting-basics.md`.

## Objectives

- Primary: identify who forged the papers and what the courier is really moving.
- Secondary: protect civilians at the checkpoint and nearby clinic.
- Optional: gain leverage with one faction without making a lasting enemy.

## Starting Scene

The player unit is posted at a dusty inspection point outside a settlement service road. A nervous courier arrives with sealed medical crates, incomplete authorization codes, and travel papers that contradict each other. A local official wants the matter handled quietly before a relief convoy arrives.

Start by asking whether the player inspects the crates, questions the courier, checks the codes, alerts the clinic, or shadows the courier.

## Key NPCs

- Len Varis, checkpoint clerk: anxious local official who wants the issue resolved without public panic.
- Sera Holt, courier: frightened, observant, and possibly carrying more than medical supplies.
- Unknown spotter: watches the checkpoint from a ridge or comms relay; identity can become a faction hook.

## Active Factions

- Local planetary authority: wants calm, plausible deniability, and uninterrupted relief traffic.
- Unknown patron: responsible for one forged paper trail.
- Unknown rival: responsible for the contradictory paper trail.

## Scene Beats

1. Briefing: the local clerk explains the checkpoint problem and asks for quiet help.
2. Inspection: the players choose how to examine papers, cargo, courier behavior, or comm traffic.
3. Complication: a second actor pressures the courier, tampers with evidence, or tries to move the crates.
4. Confrontation: resolve a chase, standoff, negotiation, or small character-scale fight.
5. Aftermath: choose who gets the crates, who gets blamed, and what hook points to the next mission.

## Rewards And Costs

- Possible rewards: local favor, faction leverage, salvage lead, medical supplies, or a contact.
- Possible costs: damaged reputation, injured NPC, missing evidence, faction suspicion, or delayed relief convoy.

## State To Track

- Which facts become table canon.
- Which faction or NPC gains trust, leverage, or resentment.
- Any PC injuries, fatigue, equipment loss, or new gear.
- Any provisional rulings, missing summaries, or awkward GM-mode steps.

## Tactical Handoff

Stay in RPG mode for questioning, investigation, a brief chase, a standoff, or a small character-scale fight. Switch to Classic BattleTech, MegaMek, or MekHQ if the scene escalates to BattleMechs, combat vehicles, meaningful terrain and facing, exact weapon range brackets, or unit-level consequences.
