# Safety And Tone

Use this file to remember player preferences and child/co-player constraints.

## Table Tone

- Tone: Confirmed by user: gritty mercenary military, not corporate or HR-polished.
- Narrative tone profile: `Rough Mercenary Military`, with `BattleTech Mercenary Realism` and `Outlaw-Country Fatalism` as secondary flavors. See `gm/narrative-tone-profiles.md`.
- Violence detail level: Serious danger and real consequences; do not linger on graphic gore unless the user explicitly raises the cap.
- Kid-friendly mode: Needs user decision.
- Profanity level: Natural soldier profanity is allowed when it fits the character and pressure.
- Humor style: Rowdy, dry, gallows, and barracks humor are preferred over polished banter.

## Dialogue And Command Voice

- Confirmed by user: leadership should use military lingo and military abstractions instead of corporate abstractions.
- Confirmed by user: officers, techs, and troops should sound rougher, rowdier, and more like mercenary soldiers than people performing HR-safe professionalism.
- Confirmed by user: style touchstones are BattleTech mercenary fiction, Sharpe-like soldiering, and the ethos of Steve Earle / Townes Van Zandt / Guy Clark / Blaze Foley style outlaw-country storytelling without copying lyrics or source text.
- Rewrite any leadership line that sounds like a corporate meeting, sanitized leadership seminar, or generic inspirational speech.
- Preferred subjects in dialogue: contracts, pay, salvage, ammo, armor, spare parts, busted machines, bad employers, dead friends, JumpShip clocks, DropShip bays, House politics, command burden, and whether the unit can still fight next week.

## Child Or Co-Player Guidance

- Do not kill a child player character without explicit adult approval.
- Favor rescue, retreat, equipment loss, time pressure, capture, or hard choices over graphic harm.
- Keep objectives visible and offer 2-4 concrete choices when a younger player stalls.
